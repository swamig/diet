# Gut Microbiome Diversification

Microbiome diversity correlates with metabolic resilience.

This protocol uses:

*   Daily fermented vegetables (2 tbsp minimum)
*   Weekly green rotation
*   Fiber variety (30–45g daily)
*   Controlled antimicrobial exposure

Strong antimicrobial spices (Clove, Ajwain) are not used daily. The objective is modulation, not sterilization.

# Hormetic Spice Stacking Framework

Spices are biologically active compounds.

High-dose daily exposure to strong antimicrobial agents (e.g., clove, ajwain) may:

*   Disrupt gut microbiota
*   Increase hepatic detox load
*   Induce adaptive downregulation

Therefore, this protocol uses rotational hormesis.

## Principles:

*   **No high-intensity antimicrobial spices are stacked on the same day.**
*   **Nigella is limited to controlled frequency.**
*   **Cacao is rotated due to oxalate and heavy metal considerations.**
*   **Saffron is micro-dosed, not culinary-dosed.**

Hormesis requires variation. Repetition without rotation reduces adaptive benefit.

# Inflammatory Signaling Control

Chronic low-grade inflammation is the underlying substrate of metabolic disease, endothelial dysfunction, and impaired recovery.

The protocol establishes a daily anti-inflammatory base layer:

*   **Turmeric:** 1/2 tsp daily
*   **Black pepper:** 1/4 tsp daily

Curcumin (the active compound in turmeric) influences multiple inflammatory cascades [[Dinkova-Kostova et al., 2012]](../../07-appendices/master-citations.md#13-metabolic-oncology--chemoprevention):

*   Downregulates NF-κB transcription activity
*   Modulates COX-2 expression
*   Reduces pro-inflammatory cytokine production

However, curcumin bioavailability is limited. Piperine (from black pepper) increases curcumin absorption by up to 2000% by inhibiting hepatic UGT glucuronidation [[Shoba et al., 1998]](../../07-appendices/master-citations.md#4-sulforaphane--inflammatory-control) and is used here as the standard culinary pairing.

**This pairing is mandatory in the protocol. It is not optional.**

This manual uses daily culinary-dose exposure as a consistent baseline layer rather than a supplement-style escalation.

## Sulforaphane Activation Protocol

Cruciferous vegetables are included daily for a specific biochemical reason: sulforaphane production.

*   Broccoli contains **glucoraphanin**.
*   **Myrosinase** (an enzyme) converts glucoraphanin into sulforaphane [[Fahey et al., 2015]](../../07-appendices/master-citations.md#4-sulforaphane--inflammatory-control).

However, myrosinase is heat-sensitive. Raw consumption yields ~74% absorption vs. ~19% from cooked broccoli [[Vermeulen et al., 2008]](../../07-appendices/master-citations.md#4-sulforaphane--inflammatory-control).

Therefore:

1.  **Broccoli is chopped.**
2.  **It rests 30 minutes before cooking.** (Non-negotiable)
3.  **Cooking time is limited to 3–4 minutes.**
4.  **Mustard powder (additional myrosinase source) is added after heat is removed** [[Okunade et al., 2018]](../../07-appendices/master-citations.md#4-sulforaphane--inflammatory-control).

Sulforaphane influences:

*   Nrf2 pathway activation [[Dinkova-Kostova et al., 2012]](../../07-appendices/master-citations.md#13-metabolic-oncology--chemoprevention)
*   Glutathione upregulation
*   Phase II detoxification enzymes

Overcooking destroys enzymatic activity and negates this pathway.

## TRP Synergy & Allicin Signaling

The protocol includes two additional biological levers for inflammation control:

1.  **TRP Receptor Cross-Talk:** Consuming high-menthol mint (*Mentha arvensis*) alongside capsaicin (chili) forces a receptor interaction that modulates systemic inflammation [[Nutrients DOI:10.3390/nu18030376]](../../07-appendices/master-citations.md#1-trp-receptor-cross-talk-menthol--capsaicin). This requires a strict 5-10 minute consumption window. See [Peppermint & Capsaicin](../02-ingredients/peppermint-chili.md).
2.  **The Allicin Rule:** Garlic must be crushed and rested for 10 minutes to activate its anti-inflammatory organosulfur compounds [[Song & Milner, 2001]](../../07-appendices/master-citations.md#3-allicin-activation-the-10-minute-garlic-rule). Jarred garlic is prohibited. See [Garlic Monograph](../02-ingredients/garlic.md).

## Conflicts

- Separating turmeric from black pepper breaks the core anti-inflammatory stack used in this manual.
- Cooking mature broccoli destroys myrosinase; microgreens bypass this entirely.
- Overcooking crucifers undermines the short-cook rule used throughout the protocol.

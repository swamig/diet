# Microgreens (Phytonutrient Amplification Layer)

## Active Compounds

Concentrated:

*   **Glucosinolates**
*   **Polyphenols**
*   **Vitamin C**
*   **Carotenoids**

Microgreens contain higher phytonutrient density per gram than mature greens.

## Dose

*   **1/2 cup daily minimum.**
*   **Emphasize radish and broccoli microgreens.**

## Protocol Role

Microgreens:

*   **Present daily**
*   **Increased on nitric oxide days**
*   **Used to diversify phytonutrient exposure**

They provide density without high oxalate load.

## Stacking Rules

- Keep microgreens present daily and increase emphasis on vascular-support days if needed.
- Treat them as a standing salad-layer requirement, not an optional flourish.

## Conflicts

- Replacing microgreens with nothing at all weakens the fresh phytonutrient layer used daily in the cycle.
- Poor storage and early spoilage turn this into a logistics problem more than a nutrition problem.

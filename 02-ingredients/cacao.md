# Cacao - Vascular Polyphenol Layer

## Active Compounds

*   **Epicatechin**
*   **Theobromine**
*   **Flavanols**

## Mechanism of Action

Cacao:

*   Enhances **nitric oxide signaling**
*   Improves **endothelial function**
*   **Mild stimulant effect**
*   Provides **magnesium**

## Risks and Constraints

*   **Oxalate content**
*   Potential heavy metal contamination

## Dose

*   1 tbsp, 2–3x weekly.
*   **Not daily.**
*   **Rotated to prevent cumulative oxalate stacking.**

## Protocol Role

- Cacao is a vascular-support rotator rather than a daily background food in this protocol.
- It is deliberately placed in the recovery context where its stimulant and oxalate tradeoffs can be managed.

## Stacking Rules

- Keep cacao to the planned 2-3x weekly rhythm used in the recovery layer.
- Treat it as a spaced rotator, not a default every-day add-on.

- Do not combine cacao nibs with chocolate whey on the same day in this protocol.
- Daily cacao use or removing the dairy / calcium context works against the intended spacing logic.

# Tomatoes (Lycopene Signaling)

Tomatoes are utilized in this protocol primarily for **Lycopene**, a potent fat-soluble carotenoid with profound cardiovascular and oncology-preventative properties.

## The Lycopene Hierarchy (Bioavailability)

Not all tomato formats provide the same signal. Bioavailability is determined by two factors: **Thermal Conversion** (Cis vs. Trans) and **Concentration**.

| Format | Lycopene Shape | Bioavailability | Protocol Role |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fresh Tomatoes** | Trans-Lycopene | **Low** | Requires aggressive cooking with fat to convert molecules. |
| **Tomato Puree** | Cis-Lycopene (Partial) | **High** | Factory heat-processing has already begun the conversion. Excellent for "Sofrito" work. |
| **Tomato Paste** | Cis-Lycopene (Max) | **Elite** | Long-term thermal reduction concentrates lycopene by 10x and maximizes bioavailability. |

## The "Sofrito" Activation Rule (Lipid-Bonding)

Lycopene is strictly fat-soluble. To absorb it, you must perform the **Sofrito Toast**:
1.  **The Bloom:** Stir your puree or paste into hot **Avocado Oil** (or EVOO) alongside your aromatics (onion/garlic/achiote).
2.  **The Result:** The heat and lipids extract the lycopene from the tomato cellular matrix and bond it to the fat molecules, allowing for intestinal absorption.

## Benefits
*   **Cardiovascular:** Supports endothelial function and reduces the oxidation of LDL cholesterol.
*   **Oncology:** Heavily cited for reducing the risk of prostate and other systemic cancers [[13]](../07-appendices/master-citations.md#13-metabolic-oncology--chemoprevention).

# Clove - High-Intensity Antimicrobial and Antioxidant Agent

## Active Compounds

*   **Eugenol**

## Mechanism of Action

Clove is a potent phenolic compound with:

*   **Strong antimicrobial activity**
*   **COX pathway modulation**
*   **High antioxidant capacity**
*   **Local anesthetic properties**

## Dose

*   1 whole clove infused in water.
*   **1–2 times per week maximum.**

## Stacking Rules

*   **Spaced and not stacked with other strong antimicrobials (e.g., nigella, ajwain).**
*   **Used during Week 2 (detox emphasis).**

## Protocol Role

- Clove is used as a high-intensity antimicrobial pulse ingredient inside the cycle.
- Its role is strongest when it is pulsed sparingly rather than normalized into the daily baseline.

## Evidence Status

- **General physiology:** The compound and mechanism descriptions on this page are used as directional context for how the ingredient may fit into the protocol.
- **Protocol-specific rules:** The exact dose, timing, pairings, weekly caps, and "mandatory" language on this page are operating rules specific to this manual.

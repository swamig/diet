# Black Pepper (Bioavailability Amplifier)

## Active Compounds

*   **Piperine**

## Mechanism of Action

Piperine:

*   Inhibits **glucuronidation**
*   Enhances absorption of **curcumin**
*   Increases intestinal permeability transiently
*   May modestly influence thermogenesis

## Dose

*   1/4 teaspoon (~0.5 grams) daily.

## Stacking Rules

Black pepper:

*   **Always paired with turmeric**
*   Does not require rotation
*   Should not be used in excessive quantity (to avoid gastric irritation)

## Protocol Role

- Black pepper is the bioavailability amplifier for the turmeric-centered anti-inflammatory layer.
- Its main role in this manual is to be added fresh at the point of use rather than treated as generic seasoning.

## Evidence Status

- **General physiology:** The compound and mechanism descriptions on this page are used as directional context for how the ingredient may fit into the protocol.
- **Protocol-specific rules:** The exact dose, timing, pairings, weekly caps, and "mandatory" language on this page are operating rules specific to this manual.

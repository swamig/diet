# Oxalate Physiology and Mineral Pairing

## The Protocol
This protocol does not eliminate oxalates. It **controls** them.

## Control Mechanisms

1.  **Rotation:** High-oxalate foods (spinach, beets, cacao) are not stacked on the same day.
2.  **Calcium Pairing:** Greek yogurt in the recovery bowl is used as a food-calcium anchor; dietary calcium can bind oxalate in the digestive tract and reduce absorption.
3.  **Hydration:** 2.5–3 liters of fluid daily reduces urinary concentration.
4.  **Green Rotation:** Spinach is excluded; kale and arugula are rotated to prevent cumulative load.

## Stacking Rules
*   **Cacao** is intentionally limited to 2–3 high-dose exposures per week.
*   **Sesame**, **Chia**, and **Cacao** are not assigned on the same day.

## Evidence Status

- **General physiology:** The interaction and load-management ideas on this page are used as conservative operating guidance inside this protocol.
- **Protocol-specific rules:** The specific exclusions, spacing windows, weekly caps, and escalation thresholds on this page are operating rules specific to this manual.

## Sources

- Kidney-stone dietary guidance on fluids, oxalate-rich foods, and adequate calcium from foods: [NIDDK, *Eating, Diet, & Nutrition for Kidney Stones*](https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/urologic-diseases/kidney-stones/eating-diet-nutrition)
- Oxalate absorption falls as daily calcium intake rises: [von Unruh et al., 2004, *Journal of the American Society of Nephrology*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15153567/)
- Dietary calcium reduces oxalate absorption and urinary oxalate excretion after oxalate loads: [Liebman and Chai, 1997, *American Journal of Clinical Nutrition*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9129476/)

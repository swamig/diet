# Source Style Guide

[Back to Appendices](README.md) | [Back to Manual](../README.md)

Use this guide when adding external support to a mechanism-heavy page.

## Core Pattern

1. Keep the page’s `Evidence Status` section.
2. Add a `Sources` section with flat bullets and stable links.
3. Leave protocol-authored rules clearly labeled as manual rules instead of pretending they came from a paper.

## Preferred Source Types

- Official health-agency guidance pages when the claim is clinical or operational.
- PubMed-indexed human trials when the claim is about human outcomes or absorption.
- High-value mechanistic papers when the claim is clearly preclinical and labeled that way.
- Stable pages such as PubMed, PMC, NIH, NCCIH, NIDDK, ODS, or a journal DOI landing page.

## Avoid

- Blogs, listicles, and unreviewed marketing pages.
- Source dumping without showing which claim cluster each source supports.
- Turning a protocol rule into a fake evidence claim.

## Bullet Format

- Use one bullet per claim cluster.
- Start with a short label like `Curcumin bioavailability:` or `Kidney stone prevention guidance:`.
- Link directly to the paper or official guidance page.

## Good Use Cases

- Absorption claims
- Safety guidance
- Human intervention results
- Mechanistic pathway descriptions that are clearly framed as mechanistic

# 3-Week Chutney Batch Session

Run this every 3 weeks to maintain fresh-tier chutney supply. Produces cilantro chutney and full coconut chutney (curry leaves blended in) — both optimized for 3-week freeze stability.

**Total active time:** ~35–40 min
**Produces:** ~24 coconut cubes + ~16 cilantro cubes per session

> For the 3-month freeze-stable versions (plain coconut base, tamarind), see [Coconut Chutney](coconut-chutney.md) and [Tamarind Chutney](tamarind-chutney.md).

---

## What You Need

### Coconut chutney (full, curry leaves blended in) — 2 Vitamix runs:

| Ingredient | Per Vitamix run | × 2 runs total |
|---|---|---|
| Fresh grated coconut (unsweetened) | **4 cups** | **8 cups total** |
| Fresh curry leaves | 3–4 tbsp packed | 6–8 tbsp |
| Green chili | 4–5 | 8–10 |
| Fresh ginger | 2 inch knob | ~180g total |
| Roasted cumin powder | 2 tsp | 4 tsp |
| Lemon juice (**Acid Lock**) | 4 tbsp | 8 tbsp |
| Water or aquafaba | 1/2 cup | 1 cup |
| Salt | 1 tsp | 2 tsp |

**Output per run:** ~3 cups = ~24 cubes
**Output × 2 runs:** ~48 cubes = 3 weeks at 1–2/day ✓

### Cilantro chutney (blanch-freeze) — 1 Vitamix run:

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Fresh cilantro (packed) | 4 cups |
| Fresh mint (packed, optional) | 2 cups |
| Thai green chilies | 4–5 |
| Garlic (smashed, rested 10 min) | 4 cloves |
| Fresh ginger | 1 inch knob |
| Lemon juice (**Acid Lock — critical**) | 4 tbsp |
| Black salt (kala namak) | 1 tsp |
| Roasted cumin powder | 1 tsp |

**Output:** ~2 cups = ~16 cubes = 3 weeks at 1/day with small buffer ✓

## Method

### Step 1 — Blanch the cilantro (5 min)

This deactivates polyphenol oxidase (PPO) — the browning enzyme — without cooking the herb or degrading the chelation compounds.

1. Bring a pot of water to a rolling boil
2. Submerge cilantro (and mint if using) for **exactly 10–15 seconds**
3. Immediately transfer to an **ice bath** — stop the cooking instantly
4. Squeeze out excess water — don't leave it waterlogged
5. Set aside

> **Why blanch:** PPO causes grey, mushy, flat-tasting frozen cilantro. 10–15 seconds deactivates PPO only — the 2-decenal and terpenoid chelation compounds are heat-stable at this exposure. Color, flavor, and function all preserved significantly better than raw-freeze.

---

### Step 2 — Blend cilantro chutney (5 min)

1. Add blanched cilantro + mint, chilies, garlic, ginger to Vitamix
2. Add lemon juice + black salt + cumin
3. Blend 60–90 sec until smooth — add a splash of water only if needed to get it moving
4. Taste: should be sharp, bright, herby, spiced. Adjust salt/lemon.
5. Pour into ice cube trays (~2 tbsp per cube)
6. Freeze immediately — don't let it sit

---

### Step 3 — Blend coconut chutney (10–12 min, 2 runs)

**Run 1:**
1. Add 4 cups grated coconut + curry leaves + chilies + ginger to Vitamix
2. Add cumin, lemon juice, salt, water/aquafaba
3. Blend 90 sec until smooth — thick paste consistency
4. Taste: rich, coconutty, sharp from ginger/chili, bright from lemon
5. Spoon into ice cube trays

**Run 2:** repeat identically with second set of ingredients

> **Vitamix note:** 4 cups grated coconut sits well under the 64oz capacity. Do not exceed 4 cups coconut per run — it blends unevenly above this.

---

### Step 4 — Freeze and label (5 min)

- Freeze all trays flat for 4 hours minimum
- Pop cubes into **separate labeled zip-lock bags:**
  - `Cilantro Chutney — [date] — use by [date +3 weeks]`
  - `Coconut Chutney Full — [date] — use by [date +3 weeks]`
- Store flat in freezer

## Shelf Life

| Chutney | Freeze stability | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Cilantro (blanched, acid-locked) | **3 weeks** | Chelation compounds ~80% intact at 3 weeks |
| Coconut full (curry leaves blended) | **3 weeks** | Curry leaf carbazole alkaloids hold well to 3 weeks |
| Coconut base (no curry leaves) | **3 months** | See [3-month base protocol](coconut-chutney.md) |
| Tamarind chutney | **3 months** | See [3-month tamarind protocol](tamarind-chutney.md) |

## At Serving Time — Fresh Herb Rule

When you thaw any cube from this batch, add fresh herbs now — **never frozen into the base:**

| Herb | Amount | What it adds |
|---|---|---|
| Fresh cilantro (chopped) | 1–2 tbsp | Live volatile top notes, additional chelation |
| Fresh mint (torn) | 4–5 leaves | Menthol cooling |
| Fresh curry leaves (torn raw) | 3–4 leaves | Raw carbazole alkaloids complementing tadka |

For the coconut chutney: still add the **fresh tadka** (mustard seeds + curry leaves in hot oil) at serving — see [Coconut Chutney §4](coconut-chutney.md#4-the-fresh-tadka-at-serving-time). The blended curry leaves in the 3-week version and the tadka at serving are complementary, not redundant.

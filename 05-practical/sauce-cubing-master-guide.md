# Sauce Cubing Master Guide

The **why** behind every sauce-cubing recipe in the protocol. [Freeze System](./freeze-system.md) is the inventory ("what freezes, in what tray, for how long"). This is the complementary design-principles doc — **how to cube a new sauce**: what goes IN the cube, what stays OUT, why, and what the math looks like.

Future sauce sheets should link back here instead of re-explaining the rules each time.

---

## 1. Why Cube Sauces

The protocol uses sauce cubing as its default delivery format for one reason: **it converts a 30-minute weeknight cook into a 5-minute thaw**, without losing the bio-signaling layer.

- **Shelf life** — concentrated sauces at low water-activity freeze for 3–6 months with minimal aroma loss.
- **Portion control** — one cube = one serving. No half-jars languishing in the fridge.
- **Low waste** — the spent water from blanching, brining, or steeping gets discarded once; the concentrate goes the distance.
- **Consistent flavor** — same blend every time. No "this batch was hotter than last week" drift.
- **Fast weeknights** — drop a cube in a pan, add water, finish off-heat. Done.
- **Concentrate-and-reduce** is the unifying mental model: pull water OUT before freezing, add it back IN at the pan.

## 2. What Goes IN the Cube

The shared property: **stable to freezing and re-warming, doesn't carry a volatile or enzyme-driven signal that dies in the freezer**.

| Category | Examples | Why it survives freezing |
|---|---|---|
| **Shelf-stable acids** | Rice vinegar, ACV, distilled white vinegar | pH already low; freeze-thaw doesn't shift acidity |
| **Salt + sugar (preservation)** | Sea salt, date paste, honey, brown sugar | Both bind water (lowers water activity); freezer-stable indefinitely |
| **Fermented condiments** | Miso, soy / tamari, oyster sauce, sriracha, gochujang | Already shelf-stable at room temp; freezing extends life further |
| **Dried / ground spices** | Turmeric, cumin, mustard, paprika, black pepper, Sichuan pepper | No water content; piperine / curcumin / capsaicin all freeze-stable |
| **Blended aromatics with lipid or acid lock** | Cilantro paste in lemon, ginger paste in oil, mint muddle in ACV | Volatile oils trapped by fat or acid layer (see [§6](#6-acid--lipid-lock-rule)) |
| **Reduced concentrates** | Shiitake broth reduced by ⅓ to ½ | Less water = less ice crystal damage, more flavor per cube |
| **Heat-stable sulfurs** | Black garlic powder, asafetida (hing) | SAC and the dried-hing sulfur compounds are heat- and freeze-stable |

## 3. What STAYS OUT (Added at the Pan or Plate)

The shared property: **signal degrades, volatilizes, or chemically attacks the cube** if frozen in.

| Category | Examples | Why it can't be frozen in |
|---|---|---|
| **Finishing oils** | Toasted sesame oil, EVOO, walnut oil, flax oil | Heat-sensitive — toasted aromatics smoke off above ~230 °F (you hit that melting a cube). Off-heat, last 30 seconds only. |
| **Raw allicin (rested garlic)** | Crushed-and-rested fresh garlic | Alliinase is destroyed by freezing AND by heat. Keep whole cloves in a Sheet 12 stash, rest day-of. |
| **Fresh herbs (top notes)** | Mint, cilantro top notes, scallion, microgreens, basil | Volatile terpenes sublime out of the cube. Torn fresh at the plate. |
| **Dilution water** | Plain water for thinning | Dead weight in the freezer — slows thaw and crowds out concentrate. Add at the pan. |
| **Salt-cured finishers** | Black salt (kala namak) | Osmotic — draws water from any paste, even frozen. Bleeds liquid and ruins texture. |
| **Fresh acid finishers** | Lemon / lime squeeze at plate | Citrus essential oils (limonene) degrade in freezer beyond 3 weeks. Cube the vinegar; squeeze the citrus. |
| **Live cultures past 130 °F** | Miso, yogurt, fresh sriracha cultures | If you want LAB to survive, the cube goes in cold or warm, never simmered. Whisk miso in off-heat. |

## 4. The Reduction Principle

The single biggest lever for cube quality is **water activity (a_w)**. Less water = better freeze stability + more concentrated flavor per cube + less freezer burn.

- **Target consistency:** glossy + thick at room temp; pourable when warm.
- **Reduction ratio:** most sauces reduce by ⅓ to ½ before cubing. Shiitake broth in the [Veg Oyster Sauce](./oyster-sauce-vegetarian.md) halves; the [Sesame Sauce](./sesame-sauce-cubes.md) doesn't reduce because all components are already low-water.
- **Don't over-reduce.** If the concentrate caramelizes during reduction, you've cooked off the volatile fermented notes you wanted to preserve. Stop at glossy-coats-a-spoon.
- **Ice-crystal damage scales with water content.** A 60% water sauce gets ~3× the freezer-burn risk of a 20% water sauce over the same shelf life.

## 5. Tray Sizing Math

Two trays handle the entire protocol. Pick by **dose granularity**: how often do you want a half-portion?

| Tray | Cell size | Sauces that go here |
|---|---|---|
| **Mini ice tray** | ~1 tsp / cell | Tadka, mint muddle, fenugreek paste, ginger paste, [Sriracha](./sriracha-homemade.md), black garlic, rested garlic |
| **Regular ice tray** | ~2 tbsp / cell | [Jaljeera](./peppermint-jaljeera.md), cilantro / coconut chutney, [Sesame Sauce](./sesame-sauce-cubes.md), [Veg Oyster Sauce](./oyster-sauce-vegetarian.md), [Imli-Khajur](./tamarind-chutney.md) |

**Choosing mini vs. regular:**
- **Mini** when one teaspoon is the meaningful dose (chili paste, ginger), or when you want to layer 2–4 small cubes in one pan without thawing too much sauce.
- **Regular** when one cube = one serving (chutneys, glazes) and you want low cube count for tray real estate.

**Cube count math:**

```
cubes_per_batch = concentrate_volume_ml ÷ cell_size_ml
batches_per_year = annual_servings ÷ cubes_per_batch
```

Worked example: [Sesame Sauce](./sesame-sauce-cubes.md) 4× batch yields ~20 tbsp ÷ 1 tbsp/cell = **20 mini cubes** ≈ 1 month at one cube per tofu meal (2×/week).

## 6. Acid + Lipid Lock Rule

The canonical case: **fresh-herb chutneys** (cilantro, mint, basil). Without a lock, you get black sludge in two weeks.

| Lock layer | What it does | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Acid** (lemon juice, ACV, lime) | Lowers pH below polyphenol oxidase (PPO) optimum — stops enzymatic browning | Cilantro chutney with 1 tbsp lemon / cup; jaljeera concentrate with 1 tbsp ACV / serving |
| **Fat** (avocado oil, EVOO sparingly) | Traps volatile aromatics (menthol, citral, terpenes) that would otherwise sublime out of the cube during freezing | Jaljeera with ½ tsp avocado oil / serving; mint muddle with ¼ tsp / cube |
| **Salt — DO NOT use as a lock** | Osmosis draws water from cells, ruins texture, doesn't stop PPO | Black salt and kala namak are **always** post-thaw additions |

For the [Jaljeera](./peppermint-jaljeera.md) 3-week cube as the worked example: 12 mint leaves + ¼ Thai chili + ginger + lemon + ACV + ½ tsp avocado oil all blend together. Black salt, cumin, and psyllium stay out — added at the glass.

## 7. Single-Use Bath Rule

Every "bath" liquid in the protocol — blanch, ice, brine, steep — is **discarded after first use**. Reusing them is the most common silent failure mode.

| Bath | Why round 2 fails |
|---|---|
| **Blanch water** | After run 1: leached chlorophyll + PPO + phenolics. The kill window for cell-wall enzymes shortens because the water is already pre-acidified by the first batch. Second-batch greens go brown faster. |
| **Ice bath** | After run 1: water has warmed past 1:1 ice:water, so the thermal shock is incomplete. PPO keeps acting in the second batch's "ice" bath. |
| **Salt brine** | After run 1: holds expelled liquid + leached compounds + surface bacteria from the first batch. Cross-contaminates the second. |
| **Shiitake steep** | Front-loaded extraction — round 1 pulls 70%+ of free glutamate and aromatic compounds. Round 2 tastes thin and earthy. Reserve the rehydrated **mushroom bodies** for the second use instead (stir-fry, chutney). |

The pattern: **the bath is single-use; the solids are multi-use.**

## 8. Pre-Freeze vs. Post-Freeze Additions

Quick-reference table for any new sauce you're designing.

| Ingredient | Pre-freeze (in cube) | Post-freeze (at pan / plate) | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Vinegar, ACV | YES | — | Stable acid, locks color |
| Soy / tamari / miso (cooked-in) | YES | — | Stable fermented base |
| Miso (live-culture) | — | YES (off-heat) | LAB dies above 130 °F |
| Dried spices | YES | — | No water; freeze-stable |
| Date paste / honey | YES | — | Binds water; preserves |
| Black pepper, cracked | YES | — | Piperine freeze-stable |
| Turmeric, cumin, paprika | YES | — | All curcuminoids stable |
| Cooked ginger paste | YES | — | Gingerol stable when blended in fat or acid |
| Rested garlic (allicin) | — | YES | Allicin destroyed by freeze + heat |
| Black salt (kala namak) | — | YES | Osmotic — bleeds water |
| Lemon / lime juice (fresh squeeze) | — | YES | Citrus oils degrade > 3 weeks frozen |
| Fresh mint, cilantro top, scallion | — | YES | Volatile oils sublime |
| Toasted sesame oil / EVOO finish | — | YES (off-heat) | Aromatics smoke off > 230 °F |
| Plain water (dilution) | — | YES | Dead freezer weight |
| Aromatics in lipid lock | YES | — | Fat traps volatiles |

## 9. The Bio-Signal Stack Pattern (Optional)

Sheets 21–23 use a consistent pattern: **base cube + day-of layer**. The base is shelf-stable, the day-of layer carries the time-sensitive signals.

- The **in-cube layer** captures everything that survives the freeze: piperine, curcumin, capsaicin, free glutamate, fermented depth.
- The **day-of layer** carries the signals that don't: raw allicin, fresh herb volatiles, off-heat finishing oil, citrus brightness.

Both layers are optional — the base sauce works without any of them — but **stacking both gives the protocol-grade meal**.

Worked examples:
- [Peppermint Jaljeera](./peppermint-jaljeera.md) — in-cube: mint/chili/ginger/lemon/ACV/oil. Day-of: black salt, cumin, psyllium, sparkling water.
- [Sesame Sauce Cubes](./sesame-sauce-cubes.md) — in-cube: soy/oyster/vinegar/sriracha/honey + optional pepper/turmeric. Day-of: rested garlic cube + toasted sesame oil + sesame seeds + torn mint.

## 10. Freeze Stability Ladder

Use this to label new sauces. **Past nominal life ≈ ~30% aroma loss but still food-safe** — repurpose into stir-fries or curries where you're adding other aromatics anyway.

| Life | Sauce category | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **3 weeks** | Fresh-herb chutneys (mint, cilantro) with lipid lock | [Jaljeera concentrate](./peppermint-jaljeera.md), [cilantro chutney](./coconut-chutney.md) |
| **4–6 weeks** | Tadka, fenugreek paste, mint muddle | Curry leaf tadka, methi paste, mint muddle cubes |
| **3 months** | Plain coconut base, beans, quinoa, ginger paste, blanched herbs, reduced glazes | Coconut chutney base, black beans, [Sesame Sauce](./sesame-sauce-cubes.md), [Veg Oyster Sauce](./oyster-sauce-vegetarian.md) |
| **4–6 months** | Combo cubes, high-acid / high-sugar sauces | [Imli-Khajur](./tamarind-chutney.md), [Homemade Sriracha](./sriracha-homemade.md) |

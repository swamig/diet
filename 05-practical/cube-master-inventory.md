## Freeze Stability Ladder

Cubes group naturally by chemistry — the table below maps ingredient categories to peak-quality freezer life. Past nominal life, most items are still safe but flavor and aromatic depth fade.

| Category | Peak quality | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Fresh-herb chutneys with lipid lock | 3 weeks | PPO eventually browns even with acid + fat |
| Tadka, fenugreek, mint muddle | 4–6 weeks | Concentrated aromatics fade after ~1 month |
| Plain bases, beans, quinoa, ginger paste, sauce concentrates | 3 months | Standard freezer life for low-water-activity items |
| Combo cubes (multi-acid + reduction) | 4 months | Acid stack + concentration extends life |
| Imli-Khajur, sriracha (high acid + sugar) | 6 months | Acid + sugar preservation push to half-year |

See [Sauce Cubing Master Guide §6 — The Freeze Stability Ladder](./sauce-cubing-master-guide.md) for the chemistry behind each tier.

## Tray Sizing Quick-Ref

The protocol uses two tray cell sizes — pick by volume.

| Tray | Cell volume | Use for |
|---|---|---|
| **Mini** | ~1 tsp | Tadka, mint muddle, fenugreek, ginger, sriracha, black garlic, sauce concentrates |
| **Regular** | ~2 tbsp | Jaljeera, chutneys, imli-khajur, large-portion sauce bases |

> **One tray of each size** is the minimum. Two of each is more comfortable — you can pour the next batch while last night's cubes finish hardening. See [freeze-system.md §Tray Count](./freeze-system.md).

---

## How to Read This Table

- **Cube size** matches the recipe — *mini* = ~1 tsp per cell, *regular* = ~2 tbsp per cell. Recipes specify which tray to use; don't substitute (a sauce concentrate poured into a 2-tbsp cell takes too long to thaw and pulls the pan temperature down too far).
- **Freeze life** is *peak quality* — past nominal life, items are still safe (~6 mo more for most) but flavor dulls ~30% and aromatic top-notes drop first. The 3-week tier (mint, cilantro) is the strict line; the 6-month tier (sriracha, imli-khajur) is the forgiving line.
- **Recipe link** opens the full instructions for that cube — batch scaling, day-of additions, what NOT to put in the cube.

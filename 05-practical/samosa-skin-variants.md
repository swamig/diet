# Samosa Skin Variants

Optional non-vegan variants for the [Functional Samosa](functional-samosas.md) skin (Options 1 & 2). The default protocol uses avocado oil + water/aquafaba. Use these when you want richer flavor, better lamination, or additional gliadin reduction.

| Variant | Fat source | Fermentation | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Ghee | No | Richer flavor, better lamination |
| B | Avocado oil | Yogurt | Gliadin reduction, pliable dough |
| C | Sour cream | Yes (built-in) | **Batch freeze prep — replaces both ghee and yogurt in one ingredient** |
| A+B combined | Ghee | Yogurt | Traditional-closest, maximum signal |

---

## Variant A: Ghee Moyen

Replace avocado oil with ghee in the day-of moyen step.

**Why ghee over avocado oil:**
- Creates better lamination layers — milk solids in ghee create a flakier, more traditional pastry texture
- Fat-soluble spice compounds (curcumin, thymoquinone, capsaicin) absorb preferentially in a saturated fat matrix — marginally higher bioavailability
- Richer flavor note that complements the spiced filling

**How to use:**
- Option 1: substitute 2 tbsp ghee for 2 tbsp avocado oil in the moyen step
- Option 2: substitute 1 tbsp ghee for 1 tbsp avocado oil
- Everything else identical — rub into dry mix until crumbly, hydrate with water/aquafaba, rest and roll

**Not vegan.** No protocol signal is lost with avocado oil — this is a flavor and texture preference.

---

## Variant B: Yogurt Fermentation

Replace roughly half the water/aquafaba with full-fat yogurt before the rest period.

**Why ferment the dough:**
- Reduces phytic acid — improves mineral absorption from the dough itself
- Lactic acid bacteria (LAB) partially degrade gliadin peptides — lower zonulin signaling vs raw unleavened dough (consistent with the gluten cycling logic in this protocol)
- Softens gluten bonds → more extensible, easier to roll thin without tearing
- Adds a subtle tang that balances the spiced filling

**Protocol:**
- Option 1: substitute 3–4 tbsp full-fat yogurt for an equal amount of cold water/aquafaba
- Option 2: substitute 3 tbsp full-fat yogurt for water
- Mix dough fully, cover tightly
- **Short ferment:** 3–4 hours at room temp — mild tang, good gliadin reduction
- **Long ferment:** overnight in the fridge — more pronounced tang, maximum gliadin degradation, dough is extremely pliable and easy to roll

**Not vegan.** Use A2 yogurt for the best LAB profile — consistent with the recovery bowl protocol.

---

## Combining Both Variants

Ghee moyen + yogurt fermentation can be used together:
1. Rub ghee into dry mix until crumbly
2. Add yogurt (3–4 tbsp) + cold water to form stiff dough
3. Ferment 3–4 hours room temp or overnight fridge
4. Roll and assemble as normal

This is the traditional-closest version of the skin — richest flavor, best lamination, lowest zonulin signal. Use on non-vegan days when you want the full sensory experience.

## Variant C: Sour Cream Fermentation (Batch Freeze Recommended)

Replaces both ghee and yogurt in one ingredient — sour cream is fermented cream, delivering fat (moyen) and live LAB cultures simultaneously.

**Why sour cream:**
- Fermented cream = high fat + LAB cultures in one step — no separate moyen needed
- LAB partially degrades gliadin peptides — same zonulin reduction as Variant B yogurt
- Cream fat adds richness and body — slightly less lamination than pure ghee but richer than avocado oil
- Ideal for KitchenAid batch prep — simplifies the wet ingredient step to one addition

**KitchenAid batch — 5 balls (conservative, recommended):**

*Dry ingredients (add to bowl first):*

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Vital Wheat Gluten (VWG) | **3¾ cups** |
| Besan (chickpea flour) | **3¾ cups** |
| Ajwain | **5 tsp** |
| Nigella | **2½ tsp** |
| Sea salt | **2½ tsp** |
| Turmeric | **1¼ tsp** |
| Black pepper | **1¼ tsp** |
| Kasuri methi | **5 tsp** |
| Black sesame | **5 tbsp** |

*Wet ingredients:*

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Full-fat sour cream | **1½ cups** |
| Cold water | **start ¾ cup — add slowly until dough just comes together** |

**KitchenAid method:**
1. Add all dry ingredients to bowl — hand-whisk briefly to combine
2. Attach dough hook, Speed 1 for 30 sec
3. Add sour cream — Speed 1 until crumbly (~1 min)
4. Add water slowly while running — stop the moment dough forms one mass (stiff, not tacky)
5. Knead Speed 2 for **5–6 minutes** until smooth and elastic
6. Remove, cover with damp cloth
7. **Ferment 3–4 hours room temp** or overnight fridge (maximum gliadin reduction)
8. Portion into **5 equal balls**, flatten into disks (~1 inch thick)
9. Wrap each in parchment → zip-lock, press out air
10. Label: `Option 1 Sour Cream — [date]` — **freeze up to 2 months**

**Dough is ready when:** smooth, slightly tacky, springs back slowly when poked.

**6-week supply (24 balls) = 4 KitchenAid runs** at 6 balls max, or **5 runs at 5 balls** (conservative). Split across 3 prep sessions (8 balls / 2 runs each, every 2 weeks).

**Not vegan.** Use full-fat sour cream for best LAB density and fat content.

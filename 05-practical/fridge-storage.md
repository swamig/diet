# Fridge Layout Optimization

**Objective:**
Maximize sulforaphane integrity and minimize nutrient degradation.

## Overall Principle
*   **Top shelf:** Ready-to-eat
*   **Middle shelf:** Proteins
*   **Bottom shelf:** Raw items
*   **Drawers:** Humidity control
*   **Door:** Condiments only

## Top Shelf (Ready & Daily Access)
*   **Pre-cooked chicken** (front left)
*   **Cooked lentils** (front right)
*   **Cooked quinoa** (center)
*   **Pre-portioned sauerkraut cups**
*   **Spice mix jars** (if refrigerated)

## Second Shelf (Greens Zone)
*   **Kale/Arugula:** Wash, spin dry, line with paper towel, seal. Replace towel midweek.
*   **Microgreens:** Keep in original vented container. Upside down with dry paper towel inside. Do not wash until use.
*   **Watercress:** Keep upright in glass with water (like herbs). Cover loosely. Change water every 2 days.

## Third Shelf (Yogurt & Recovery)
*   Greek yogurt tub
*   Casein jars
*   Thawed blueberries

## Crisper Drawers
*   **High Humidity:** Broccoli (unchopped lasts longer), Kale, Romaine.
*   **Low Humidity:** Ginger, Lemons, Green onions (wrapped in dry towel).

## Bottom Shelf (Raw Protein)
*   Raw chicken (sealed container).
*   Tofu (sealed until use, then submerged in water).

## Door (Do NOT Store Produce Here)
*   Balsamic vinegar
*   Mustard
*   Hot sauce

## Midweek Reset (Wed 5 Min)
1.  Replace damp paper towels.
2.  Chop fresh broccoli.
3.  Rotate microgreens to front.
4.  Check tofu water.

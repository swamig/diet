# Veg Oyster + Sriracha Combo Cubes

A pre-combined cube of the [Vegetarian Oyster Sauce](./oyster-sauce-vegetarian.md) (heat-stable umami) and [Homemade Sriracha](./sriracha-homemade.md) (capsaicin heat layer). When a recipe — Sheet 21 sesame sauce, a stir-fry finish, a noodle bowl — would call for both, **one combo cube replaces two**.

Built from scratch in one session rather than blending the two existing concentrates: the all-at-once reduction concentrates acid + salt + date sugar together, which gives noticeably better freeze stability than melting two finished cubes back together.

## Base Recipe (single batch · ~16 mini cubes · 4-month freeze)

| Ingredient | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Dried shiitake mushrooms | 1 oz (~30 g) | **Whole caps**, not slices — slices over-extract |
| Hot water (for steep) | 2 cups | Just-off-boil |
| Red Fresno OR red jalapeño | 8 oz (~225 g) | Stemmed · **not seeded** |
| Garlic cloves | 4–6 | Peeled |
| Fresh ginger | 1 thumb | Rough chop |
| Tamari / soy sauce | 3 tbsp | Tamari = gluten-free |
| Medjool dates blended w/ 1 tbsp warm water | 2 dates | Date-paste swap for refined sugar |
| Rice vinegar | 1 tbsp | Umami acidity |
| Distilled white vinegar | 2 tbsp | Preservation + sriracha character |
| Sea salt | 1 tsp | Adjust after reduce |
| Arrowroot slurry | 1 tbsp + 2 tbsp water | Glossy thickener — freezes cleaner than cornstarch |

## Method (~50 min hands-on)

1. **Steep.** Pour just-off-boil water over the dried shiitakes. Steep **30 min** until plump. Strain — **reserve the mushroom caps** for stir-fry, cilantro chutney, or rice (see [Veg Oyster Sauce](./oyster-sauce-vegetarian.md) for the no-waste pattern).
2. **Blend.** Combine shiitake liquid + peppers + garlic + ginger + tamari + date paste + both vinegars + salt in the blender. Blend smooth.
3. **Reduce.** Pour into a saucepan. Simmer **15–20 min** on medium-low until volume drops by ~⅓ and the sauce coats the back of a spoon.
4. **Thicken.** Whisk in the arrowroot slurry. Cook 60–90 sec until glossy.
   - **Don't boil hard** — arrowroot breaks above 175 °F sustained.
5. **Finish.** Off heat. Stir in any optional bio-signals from the table below. Cool completely.
6. **Cube.** Pour ~1 tbsp per cell into a mini ice cube tray. Freeze overnight uncovered, transfer to a labeled bag.

Label: `Oyster+Sriracha Combo — [date] — use by [date + 4 months]`. Yields ~16 cubes.

## Optional Bio-Signal Stack

All optional — recipe works without any of them.

| Layer | Add | Per batch | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Piperine** | Black pepper, coarse-cracked | ½ tsp | Absorption multiplier for everything else in the meal |
| Curcumin | Turmeric | ¼ tsp | Nrf2 pulse, paired with black pepper |
| **TRPV1 diversity** | Smoked paprika | 1 tsp | Different capsaicinoid profile + smoky note |
| TRPA1 | Black mustard seeds, toasted & ground | ½ tsp | Adds TRPA1 alongside the capsaicin + sulforaphane precursors |
| Heat-stable sulfur | Black garlic powder | 1 tsp | SAC for sustained cardiovascular signaling |
| Fermented depth | White miso | 1 tbsp | Whisk in off-heat after the reduction (preserves live cultures) |

## Day-of Additions (NOT in the cube)

| Add | Per serving | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Toasted sesame oil** | 1 tsp | Toasted aromatics degrade above ~230 °F — finishing oil only |
| Water | 2 tbsp | Loosens the cube into a glaze |
| **Rested garlic cube** ([Sheet 12](./freeze-system.md)) | 1 cube | Raw allicin pulse — doesn't survive freezing |
| Fresh mint, torn | 5–6 leaves | TRPV1 / TRPM8 synergy on tofu day |
| Toasted sesame seeds | 1 tbsp | Lignans + visual finish |

## Use Pattern

- **In [Sesame Sauce Cubes](./sesame-sauce-cubes.md):** 1 combo cube replaces 1 oyster + 1 sriracha cube in the base recipe.
- **As a standalone tofu glaze:** 1 cube + 2 tbsp water + 1 tsp toasted sesame oil at the pan. Toss crispy tofu, finish with sesame + mint.
- **Stir-fry finisher:** 1 cube + 2 tbsp water over warm vegetables.
- **Saucy noodles:** 2 combo cubes + 3 tbsp water + 1 tsp toasted sesame oil.

## Optional Fermented Variant

For more flavor complexity, replace step 1: ferment the peppers + garlic + 1 tsp salt for 5–7 days first (Mode A from [Homemade Sriracha](./sriracha-homemade.md)). Then steep the shiitakes, blend the fermented mash with the rest, simmer, and cube as above. Adds ~30% flavor depth at a 5-day wait — and the live LAB cultures are killed in the reduce, so the upside is purely flavor, not probiotic.

## Critical Rules (inherited from parent sheets)

- **Sesame oil + water never go in the cube** — both added at the pan. Sesame oil's toasted aromatics smoke off above ~230 °F; water is dead weight in the freezer.
- **Rested-garlic allicin pulse stays separate.** Crushed-and-rested only; alliinase doesn't survive freezing or sustained heat. Pull a [Sheet 12](./freeze-system.md) garlic cube off-heat at the end.
- **Single-use shiitake steeping water.** Don't reuse across batches — umami extracts front-loaded, and round 2 tastes like dirt. Reserve the mushroom bodies instead.
- **Date paste at 2 dates / batch** keeps the per-cube glycemic load negligible (~⅛ date per cube).
- **Don't seed the peppers.** Seeds and pith carry most of the capsaicin — seeding cuts heat by ~70% and removes the TRPV1 signaling target.

## Freeze Stability

- **4 months** at -18 °C (standard chest freezer).
- The combo cube actually beats either parent cube individually because the in-pot reduction + acid concentration + salt + date sugar together do more preservation work than either alone — the lower water activity (pH ~3.8, ~2.5% salt, concentrated solids) puts it ahead of the 3-month veg oyster cube and matches the 6-month sriracha cube's preservation chemistry while keeping the umami body.
- Cubes are individually quick-frozen on the tray, then bagged — no clumping if flat-frozen before bagging.

## What This Replaces in Practice

Two cubes pulled from two separate bags, plus the mental overhead of remembering both. The combo cube collapses the heat + umami layer into a single grab — useful on Sheet 21 sesame sauce nights, tofu glaze nights, and any stir-fry where you'd default to "soy + sriracha + something mushroom-y." Keep the parent cubes ([Veg Oyster](./oyster-sauce-vegetarian.md), [Sriracha](./sriracha-homemade.md)) for the cases where you want only one of the two signals (refried beans wants oyster only; jaljeera wants neither; recovery bowl wants neither).

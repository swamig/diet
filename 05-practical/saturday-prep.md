# Saturday: The Initial Cook & First Chill

This is the second step of the **Elite 72-Hour Weekend Timeline**. In this phase, we gelatinize the starch and immediately trigger the formation of **Resistant Starch Type 3 (RS3)**.

## The Objective
To cook the beans thoroughly and initiate **Retrogradation** through rapid cooling.

## The Workflow

1.  **Drain & Rinse:** Drain the soaking water from the Friday bowls and rinse the beans one final time.
2.  **The Infusion Boil (90 Min):**
    *   Place beans in separate large stainless steel pots with fresh filtered water.
    *   **Flavor Base:** Add 1 tsp whole Cumin seeds and 1/2 tsp Ajwain to the pots.
    *   **The Boil:** Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Cook for 60-90 minutes until tender but not falling apart.
3.  **The Aquafaba Reserve (CRITICAL):** Before draining, reserve **1/2 cup of the thick boiling liquid** from the chickpeas. Store this in a mini glass jar in the fridge. This is your emulsifier for [Toum](functional-indian-formats.md#2-high-signaling-emulsions-toum--garlic-paste) and Patty dough.
4.  **The Rapid Cool:** Drain the remaining liquid. Immediately spread the hot beans in a single layer onto flat **stainless steel baking sheets**. 
    *   *Why:* Spreading them flat allows the heat to escape instantly, stopping the cooking process and triggering the starch molecules to begin realigning into crystals.
5.  **The First Chill:** Once the steam has stopped, place the trays directly into the fridge. Leave for **24 hours**.

## Signaling Goal
This 24-hour cold exposure locks in the initial crystalline matrix of the starch [[7]](../07-appendices/master-citations.md#7-gut-microbiome--resistant-starch-type-3). You are building the foundation of the protocol's glycemic control.

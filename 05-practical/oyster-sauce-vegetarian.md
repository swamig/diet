# Vegetarian Oyster Sauce

A protocol-compatible mushroom-based stand-in for commercial oyster sauce. Dried shiitake broth reduced into a glossy concentrate; soy + date paste for body; arrowroot for thickener. The spent shiitake bodies are reserved for chutney or stir-fry — nothing wasted, no animal product, single-use steeping water rule applies.

Drops straight into [Sesame Sauce Cubes](./sesame-sauce-cubes.md) as a 1:1 swap.

## Base Recipe (single batch · ~12 cubes · ~1 cup concentrate)

| Ingredient | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Dried shiitake mushrooms | 1 oz (~30 g) | **Whole caps**, not slices — slices over-extract |
| Hot water | 2 cups | Just-off-boil · steeping water becomes the umami broth |
| Soy sauce / tamari | ¼ cup | Tamari = gluten-free |
| Date paste | 2 Medjool dates blended | Or 2 tbsp brown sugar, or 2 tbsp maple — date paste is the protocol default |
| Rice vinegar | 1 tbsp | Unseasoned |
| White or chickpea miso | 1 tbsp | **Off heat — whisk in after reduction** to preserve LAB cultures and fermented depth |
| Arrowroot slurry | 1 tbsp + 2 tbsp water | Or cornstarch · arrowroot freezes cleaner |
| Salt | ¼ tsp | Adjust to taste at the end of the reduce |

## Method

1. **Steep.** Pour just-off-boil water over the dried shiitakes in a heat-safe bowl. Cover, steep **30 min** until plump.
2. **Strain & reserve.** Lift the caps out — squeeze gently. **Reserve the mushroom bodies** for stir-fry, cilantro chutney, or rice. Strain the steeping liquid through a fine mesh to catch any grit.
3. **Build the broth.** In a saucepan, combine strained mushroom broth + soy + date paste + rice vinegar. Bring to a simmer.
4. **Reduce.** Simmer uncovered **~15 min** until volume halves. Should coat the back of a spoon.
5. **Thicken.** Whisk in the arrowroot slurry. Cook 60–90 sec until glossy and thick.
   - **Don't boil hard** — arrowroot breaks above 175 °F sustained.
6. **Finish.** Off heat. Salt to taste. Cool completely before portioning.
7. **Cube.** Pour ~1 tbsp per cell into mini ice cube tray. Freeze overnight, transfer to labeled bag.

Label: `Veg Oyster — [date] — use by [date + 3 months]`. Yields ~12 cubes from 1 cup concentrate.

## Optional Bio-Signal Stack

All optional — recipe works without any of them.

| Layer | Add | Per batch | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Free glutamate** | Strip of dried kombu | 1 × 2-inch strip | Steeped with shiitakes for an extra MSG-like umami punch. Pull before reducing. |
| Piperine | Black pepper, fresh-cracked | ½ tsp | Absorption multiplier for the meal |
| Star-anise note | Star anise (whole) | 1 point | Steeped with shiitakes · pull before blending |
| Smoked depth | Black garlic powder | 1 tsp | SAC for heat-stable sulfur signaling |
| Cinnamon bridge | Cinnamon stick (small) | 1 stick | Steeped with the shiitakes for a faint warm undernote — pull before reducing |
| Vegan thickener swap | Cornstarch instead of arrowroot | — | Thicker mouthfeel; less freeze-stable (cornstarch gel weeps after thaw) |

## Critical Rules

- **Single-use steeping water.** Don't reuse the shiitake liquid across batches — the umami compounds extract front-loaded, and round 2 tastes like dirt. Reserve the mushroom caps instead; that's where the second use lives.
- **Arrowroot vs cornstarch.** Arrowroot stays glossy through freeze-thaw; cornstarch gel weeps water after thaw and the sauce separates. If you're going to freeze, use arrowroot.
- **Cool before cubing.** Pouring hot concentrate into silicone trays cracks the silicone over time and the trays warp.

## Spent Shiitake Bodies — Don't Waste

After the 30-min steep, the rehydrated mushroom caps still hold ~30% of their flavor. Two protocol-compatible ways to use them:

1. **Stir-fry add-in.** Slice and toss into the Sheet 07 tofu pan during the last 60 seconds — they absorb the sesame glaze beautifully.
2. **Cilantro chutney boost.** Add 2–3 caps to the next [Sheet 11 cilantro chutney](./coconut-chutney.md) blend. Adds an unexpected umami note without changing the chutney's character.

## Freeze Stability

- **3 months** at -18 °C.
- Cubes go into bags after individual flat-freeze on the tray.

## Use Pattern

- In [Sesame Sauce Cubes](./sesame-sauce-cubes.md): 1:1 swap for the commercial oyster sauce line in the base recipe.
- As a stir-fry finisher: drop 1 cube into a pan over warm vegetables, melt with 1 tbsp water.
- In refried beans / black bean prep (Sheet 14): adds umami depth to the day-of allicin pulse.

## What This Replaces

Commercial oyster sauce typically contains: oyster extract, sugar (often #1 ingredient by mass), water, salt, cornstarch, caramel coloring, MSG, sodium benzoate, and frequently wheat. The mushroom version replaces all of these with:

- Real shiitake-derived glutamate (free L-glutamate from dried mushrooms, ~5–10× higher than fresh)
- Date paste (whole-fruit fiber + minerals replacing refined sugar)
- Tamari (gluten-free, fermented soy)
- Arrowroot (clean tuber starch)
- Zero preservatives or coloring agents

# Protocol Equipment

Recommended equipment for the 3-month batch prep cycle. All items selected for affordability, compatibility with the protocol's cooking constraints (induction, 130°F ceiling, large batch volumes), and dishwasher/sink clearance.

---

## Batch Cooking — Stock Pots

**4× [Winware SST-24 Stainless Steel 24 Qt Stock Pot with Cover](https://www.amazon.com/Winware-Stainless-Steel-Quart-Silver/dp/B001AS90CA)**

| Spec | Detail |
|---|---|
| Capacity | 24 quarts |
| Material | 18/8 stainless steel |
| Base diameter | ~9–10 inches |
| Induction compatible | Yes (NSF certified) |
| Approximate price | ~$40–50 each / ~$160–200 for 4 |

**Why 4 pots:**
- 2 pots chickpeas + 2 pots black beans run simultaneously — full 3-month batch in one session
- Chickpea and black bean liquid must never mix (separate aquafaba vs anthocyanin extract)
- After beans: reuse pots for lentils and quinoa (sequential, same day)

**Pot usage map:**
| Pot | Contents | Liquid yield |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Chickpeas batch A (~2.5 kg dry) | Aquafaba |
| 2 | Chickpeas batch B (~2.5 kg dry) | Aquafaba |
| 3 | Black beans batch A (~2.25 kg dry) | Black bean liquid |
| 4 | Black beans batch B (~2.25 kg dry) | Black bean liquid |

**Cleaning:** Hand-wash only — too tall for most dishwashers. Rinse while still warm. Wash chickpea pots before black bean pots (black bean liquid stains).

## Batch Cooking — Induction Burners

**4× [ChangBERT 1800W Commercial Grade Induction Burner](https://www.amazon.com/ChangBERT-Commercial-Grade-Countertop-NSF-certificated-Professional/dp/B0B4J6PXPN)**

| Spec | Detail |
|---|---|
| Power | 1800W |
| Coil diameter | 8 inches |
| Efficiency | ~85–90% vs gas ~40–55% |
| NSF certified | Yes |
| Approximate price | ~$40–45 each / ~$160–180 for 4 |

**Why induction over gas:**
- 85–90% efficiency vs 40–55% gas — significant savings on a 90–120 min 4-pot batch cook
- Kitchen stays cooler — no ambient heat from open flame
- Precise temperature control — easier to maintain a gentle simmer for RS3 preservation

**Critical electrical requirement:**
- Each burner draws ~15 amps at 1800W
- **Each burner must be on its own circuit** — never plug 2 burners into the same circuit or you will trip the breaker
- Standard US kitchen has 2–4 circuits — check yours before batch day
- If only 2 circuits available: run 2 pots at a time, ~2 extra hours total

**Pot-to-burner fit note:** The 24-qt pot base (9–10 inches) overhangs the 8-inch coil slightly. Induction still couples effectively — the electromagnetic field covers the full pot base. No issue.

## Emulsification — Immersion Blender

**[Braun MultiQuick 5 Immersion Hand Blender (MQ505)](https://www.amazon.com/Braun-MQ505-Multiquick-Blender-Black/dp/B01EA5ZI2U)**

| Spec | Detail |
|---|---|
| Power | 350W |
| Speeds | Dual speed + turbo |
| Includes | Beaker + whisk attachment |
| Approximate price | ~$40–50 |

**Protocol uses:**
- **Toum base cubes** — emulsifies rested garlic + lemon + aquafaba into a stable emulsion. The aquafaba saponins act as the natural emulsifier; the immersion blender builds the stable oil-in-water structure in 2–3 minutes
- **TRP Chelation Chutney** — blends frozen cilantro, mint, chilies, garlic directly from frozen
- **Coconut chutney base** — blends frozen grated coconut into base
- **Chutneys + sauces** — any recipe requiring smooth emulsification in a tall beaker

**Why immersion over countertop blender for toum:**
- Toum emulsification works better in a tall narrow beaker (included) — the blade stays submerged in the emulsion as it builds, preventing air incorporation that breaks the structure
- Faster cleanup — one wand vs a full blender jar
- The aquafaba hack (2 tbsp in the beaker before adding oil) stabilizes the emulsion and prevents separation in the fridge

## Cooling & Portioning

**Half-sheet pans (18"×13") — at least 4**

Required for simultaneously cooling chickpeas, black beans, sweet potato, and samosa veg after cooking. Without enough sheet pans, you can't spread food in a single layer — stacked food traps heat and overshoots the RS3 temperature window. Standard half-sheet pans (~$10–15 each).

**Instant-read probe thermometer — 1**

Two protocol uses:
- Verify RS3 reheat ceiling ≤130°F (54°C) when warming legumes and quinoa at serving time
- Check aquafaba temperature before portioning into cube trays (must be below 40°C to avoid warping silicone)

Recommended: Thermapen ONE or any fast-read probe (~$30–100).

## Cold Storage

**Second full-size refrigerator — 1**

The 3-month batch produces ~37kg of food requiring simultaneous 12–24h RS3 chill before freezing. A single fridge cannot hold this alongside weekly fresh food. A second fridge (dedicated batch fridge) eliminates the RS3 bottleneck entirely and collapses the 3-month session from a 2-day split into a single day.

| Option | Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Used full-size fridge (Craigslist/FB Marketplace) | ~$50–150 | Best value — garage unit |
| New compact fridge (4.4 cu ft) | ~$150–200 | Fits ~20kg flat-stacked — covers beans only; need 2-day split for full batch |
| New full-size fridge (18+ cu ft) | ~$400–600 | Covers entire 37kg batch simultaneously — single day |

**Recommended:** Used full-size fridge in garage or utility space. Pays for itself in the first batch session (eliminates the 2-day split, saves ~2–3h).

**Chest freezer — 1**

The protocol's 3-month frozen stash (chickpeas, black beans, tofu, quinoa, sweet potato, lentils + all cube containers) will fill a standard kitchen freezer completely, leaving no room for ice, daily items, or the RS3 chill cycle. A chest freezer holds the bulk stash and frees the kitchen freezer for daily use and cube trays.

| Size | Capacity | Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5 cu ft | ~140 liters | ~$150–200 | Tight for a full 3-month batch |
| 7 cu ft | ~200 liters | ~$200–250 | Comfortable for all proteins + cubes |
| 10 cu ft | ~280 liters | ~$250–350 | Ideal — room for full batch + buffer |

**Recommended:** 7 cu ft chest freezer. Covers the full batch with room for expansion.

## Cube Trays

**Silicone ice cube trays (covered, 15-cube) — 11 minimum, 14 recommended**

The 3-month session fills trays simultaneously as follows:

| Session | Items | Trays needed |
|---|---|---|
| 3-month day | Aloe vera (5 trays) + Lemon juice (4 trays) + Imli (1 tray) + Aquafaba (1 tray minimum) | **11** |
| Monthly | Ginger (2) + Rested garlic (1) + Toum base (1) + Curry leaf tadka (1) + Mint muddle (2) + Fenugreek paste (1) | **8** |
| Sunday | Coconut chutney (1) + Cilantro/mint chutney (1) | **2** |

With 8 trays (original recommendation) you cannot fill all 3-month items simultaneously — aloe and lemon have to be done in shifts overnight. **With 11 trays you fill everything in one go and transfer overnight.** 14 trays gives 3 spare for monthly session overlap.

Covered silicone trays prevent freezer odour absorption and allow stacking. ~$8–12 per tray.

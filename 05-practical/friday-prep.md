# Friday Evening: The Anti-Phytic Soak

This is the first step of the **Elite 72-Hour Weekend Timeline**. The goal of this phase is to neutralize the nutritional inhibitors found in dried legumes before cooking.

## The Objective
To reduce **Phytic Acid** (which blocks mineral absorption) and **Lectins**, while hydrating the starch molecules for a more uniform gelatinization during the Saturday cook.

## Ingredients
*   **Dried Chickpeas** (2-2.5 kg).
*   **Dried Black Beans** (1 kg).
*   **Filtered Water.**
*   **Sea Salt** (1 tsp per large bowl).

## The Workflow
1.  **Rinse:** Thoroughly rinse the dried beans in a strainer until the water runs clear.
2.  **The Submersion:** Place chickpeas and black beans into separate, large glass or stainless steel bowls.
3.  **Hydrate:** Fill with filtered water until the water level is at least 3-4 inches above the beans (they will expand significantly).
4.  **The Catalyst:** Add 1 tsp of sea salt to each bowl. This helps soften the skins and assists in the breakdown of complex sugars.
5.  **The Wait:** Leave on the counter (room temperature) for **at least 12-16 hours**.

## Signaling Goal
By neutralizing the phytic acid tonight, you ensure that the **Iron-Reduction Anchor** (Aamla/Lemon) in your Monday lunch will actually be able to pull the iron into your bloodstream, rather than being bound by phytates.

# Master Batch Prep

Everything in one place: what to buy, how much, and how to make it. Organized by cadence so you can execute each session start to finish without switching between documents.

---

## Assumptions (confirm/adjust)
- 1 person
- 4 patty days/week (2 patties per day + 1/2 cup chickpeas alongside)
- 2 tofu days/week (200g per serving)
- 1 lentil/other day per week
- Black beans: ~3 days/week, 1.5 cups cooked per day (alternates with chickpeas as the legume base)
- Quinoa: average 1/2 cup cooked/day
- Purple sweet potato: 2 days/week as quinoa swap, 150g per serving

---

| Cadence | What you do | Time |

| **Monthly** | Top up cube trays (garlic, ginger, muddle, tadka, fenugreek) from stash | ~30 min |
| **Every 2–3 weeks (Sunday)** | Patties + chutneys (coconut base + cilantro/mint) | Normal Sunday prep |

### How to Prep — Cadence 1

---

**Chickpeas**
1. **Friday night:** Rinse 5 kg dried chickpeas. Soak in a large pot with 3× water overnight.
2. **Saturday:** Drain and rinse. Cook in fresh water with 2 halved onions and 1 head of garlic (whole, halved crosswise). Boil 90 min until fully tender but not mushy.
3. **Last 15 min:** Add a large knob of ginger (no need to peel). Remove after cooking.
4. **Reserve aquafaba:** Before draining, scoop out 2–3 cups of cooking liquid into a jar. Freeze aquafaba in ice cube tray separately (2 tbsp per cube — use for patties, Toum, and refried beans).
5. **Cool:** Spread chickpeas on a large tray in a single layer. Cool at room temp 20–30 min.
6. **Portion:** Measure into 1.5-cup portions in freezer bags. Squeeze out air, seal flat.
7. **Label:** Write date + "chickpeas 1.5C" on each bag.
8. **Chill:** Place in fridge overnight (12–24h RS3 formation — mandatory before freezing).
9. **Freeze:** Transfer to freezer flat.

---

**Black Beans**
Identical to chickpeas. Cook separately (different flavor profile — keep batches separate).
- Same soak, cook, aquafaba reserve, cool, portion, chill, freeze sequence.
- Black bean cooking liquid is darker — save separately as black bean aquafaba.

---

**Lentils**
1. No soaking required (optional 30 min speeds cook time).
2. Rinse. Cook in fresh water 20–25 min until tender.
3. Add ginger knob for last 10 min. Remove.
4. Drain, spread on tray, cool 20 min.
5. Portion into 1.5-cup bags, chill 12h, freeze flat.

---

**Quinoa**
1. Rinse thoroughly under cold water for 2 min (removes bitter saponin coating — do not skip).
2. Cook: 1 cup dry quinoa + 2 cups water. Bring to boil, reduce to simmer, cover 15 min. Remove from heat, steam covered 5 min.
3. Spread on a tray, cool 20 min.
4. Portion into 1/2-cup cooked servings in freezer bags.
5. Chill 12–24h in fridge (RS3 formation).
6. Freeze flat.

---

**Purple Sweet Potato**
1. Scrub skins — do not peel. Skin contains the highest concentration of anthocyanins.
2. Steam or roast whole at 400°F (200°C) until just tender (30–40 min depending on size).
3. Cool completely, then refrigerate for **24h minimum** (RS3 formation — mandatory).
4. After 24h: either leave whole or dice into cubes (skin on).
5. Bag in portion sizes (150g per bag), freeze flat.

---

**Tofu**
1. Open packages, drain liquid. Do not press — freezing handles moisture.
2. Slice into 1cm slabs (thickness of one finger).
3. Pat lightly dry.
4. Lay in a single layer in a freezer bag — do not stack wet slabs.
5. Squeeze out air, seal.
6. Freeze **flat**. Once frozen solid (2–3 hours), bags can be stacked.

---

**Lemon Juice Cubes**
*~50 lemons → ~6 trays*
1. Use a manual citrus press — fastest for volume.
2. Juice all lemons, strain out seeds (keep pulp if you want — adds pectin).
3. Pour into covered ice cube trays (~1 tbsp per cube = juice of ~1/2 lemon).
4. Freeze overnight.
5. Transfer to a labeled airtight bag: "Lemon juice — juiced [date]."
6. **Shelf life frozen:** 3–4 months.

---

**Aloe Vera Cubes**
*5 large leaves → ~5 trays (~65 cubes)*
1. **Drain the latex first:** Cut each leaf at the base. Stand upright cut-end down in a glass or bowl for 10–15 min. Yellow latex (aloin) drains out — do not skip. Aloin is bitter and irritates skin.
2. Lay each leaf flat. Slice off the serrated edges on both sides with a sharp knife.
3. **Fillet the top skin:** Run knife just under the flat green surface and peel it back. Clear gel is exposed underneath.
4. Scoop out gel with a spoon — stay away from the skin edge where residual yellow latex sits.
5. Blend all gel until completely smooth.
6. Pour into ice cube trays (~1 tbsp per cube).
7. Freeze overnight, transfer to labeled airtight container.
8. **Shelf life frozen:** 3–4 months.
9. **Use:** 1 cube per hair mask, 1 per face mask, 1 per body ubtan.

---

**Imli (Tamarind) Cubes**
*Makes ~1 tray (~14 cubes)*
1. Whisk 3–4 tbsp tamarind concentrate into 1/2 cup warm water until smooth (syrup consistency).
2. Stir in 1/2 tsp roasted cumin powder + 1/8 tsp Ceylon cinnamon.
3. **No salt** — osmosis rule. Add black salt fresh at serving.
4. Cool completely to room temp.
5. Pour into covered ice cube tray.
6. Freeze overnight, transfer to labeled container.
7. **Shelf life frozen:** 2–3 months.
8. **To use:** Thaw 1–2 cubes at room temp 15 min. Add pinch black salt + extra cumin if needed.

---

**Chilies — All Varieties (Thai Bird's Eye, Green, Habanero)**
1. Wash, dry completely — moisture causes freezer burn.
2. Place in a freezer bag, remove air, seal.
3. Freeze as-is. No blanching needed.
4. **To use:** Add directly from frozen to hot pan. For muddle, thaw 10 min at room temp first.

---

**Garlic (Peeled Cloves)**
1. Peel all cloves (~25 heads — do this in front of the TV).
2. Spread on a tray in a single layer. Freeze 1–2 hours until solid.
3. Transfer to an airtight bag or container (pre-freezing prevents clumping).
4. Pull individual cloves as needed — they thaw in minutes.

---

**Ginger (Whole Knobs)**
1. Wash. Do not peel.
2. Freeze whole in a sealed bag.
3. **To use:** Grate directly from frozen (the skin grates off naturally, or peel while semi-frozen — skin slips off easily).
4. **Shelf life frozen:** 3 months.

---

**Cilantro (Bunches)**
1. Wash thoroughly, spin or pat dry completely — excess water = ice crystals = freezer burn.
2. Blanch in boiling water for **10 seconds only** (stops enzymatic browning).
3. Immediately transfer to an ice water bath for 30 seconds.
4. Drain, pat completely dry.
5. Lay flat in freezer bags, remove air, seal.
6. **To use:** Drop frozen directly into blender when making TRP Chelation Chutney.

---

**Mentha Arvensis / Mint (Bunches)**
Same process as cilantro — wash, blanch 10 sec, ice bath, dry, bag flat.

---

**Curry Leaves (For Tadka Cubes)**
1. Wash, dry thoroughly (no blanching — they're used cooked).
2. Spread on a tray, freeze 1 hour.
3. Transfer to an airtight bag.
4. **To use:** Pull a handful when making monthly tadka cubes — no thawing needed.

---

**Fresh Coconut (Grated)**
1. Grate fresh coconut or buy pre-grated unsweetened.
2. Spread on a tray, freeze 1 hour (prevents clumping).
3. Transfer to an airtight bag in loose portions.
4. **To use:** Measure frozen directly into blender for coconut chutney base.

## Cadence 2 — Monthly (~30 min)

Pull ingredients from the 3-month freezer stash. Make all 5 cube types in one session.

---

**Ginger Cubes**
*Makes 1–2 trays (~14–28 cubes) | Shelf life frozen: 3 months*
1. Pull 2–3 ginger knobs from the freezer. Let sit 5 min — semi-frozen is easiest to peel.
2. Peel (skin slips off easily when semi-frozen).
3. Grate on a fine grater or blend with minimal water into a smooth paste.
4. Portion into ice cube tray (~1 tbsp per cube).
5. Freeze overnight, transfer to labeled bag.
6. **Daily use:** 1 cube = 1 tbsp = ~1 daily thumb. Drop into a warm pan or add to recipes directly.

---

**Rested Garlic Cubes**
*Makes 1 tray (~14 cubes) | Shelf life frozen: 4–6 weeks peak; 3 months acceptable*
1. Pull ~20 frozen garlic cloves from the freezer. Thaw 10 min.
2. Smash and mince finely.
3. **Rest 10 minutes** — non-negotiable. This is when alliinase converts alliin to allicin. Set a timer.
4. After rest, add 4–5 drops avocado oil to bind.
5. Portion into ice cube tray (~1 tsp per cube).
6. Freeze overnight, transfer to labeled container.
7. **CRITICAL:** Never freeze before the 10-minute rest. Ice crystals damage alliinase — thawed whole garlic cannot form allicin. The rest must happen before the freeze.

---

**Curry Leaf Tadka Cubes**
*Makes 1 tray (~14 cubes) | Shelf life frozen: 3–4 weeks peak; 6 weeks maximum*
1. Heat 4 tbsp avocado oil in a small pan on medium.
2. Add 1/2 tsp mustard seeds. Wait for them to pop (30–60 sec).
3. Immediately add a large handful of frozen curry leaves — they will spit and crackle. Stand back.
4. Bloom 20–30 seconds until fragrant and slightly crisp. Do not over-cook.
5. Remove from heat. Cool **completely** — hot oil in an ice tray cracks the tray.
6. Spoon into ice cube tray (~1 tsp oil + leaves per cube).
7. Freeze overnight, transfer to labeled container.
8. **To use:** Drop 1 cube directly into a warm pan or pour melted over thawed coconut chutney.

---

**Mint Muddle Cubes**
*Makes 1–2 trays (~14–28 cubes) | Shelf life frozen: 4–6 weeks peak*
1. Pull frozen mint, Thai chilies, and ginger from freezer. Thaw mint and chili 10 min.
2. Place mint leaves + 1 Thai chili + 1 tsp grated ginger into mortar and pestle.
3. Crush vigorously until fully pulverized — must rupture all cell walls to release menthol. Takes 2–3 min of real effort.
4. **Oil & Acid Lock (mandatory):** Add 1 tsp avocado oil + 1 tbsp lemon juice while continuing to muddle. The acid halts enzymatic browning, the fat traps volatile menthol.
5. **No salt, no cumin, no black salt** — osmosis bleeds water from paste even frozen.
6. Portion into ice cube tray (1 tbsp per cube).
7. Freeze overnight, transfer to labeled container.
8. **To use:** Drop 1 cube into glass, thaws in cold water in 5–10 min. Add black salt, cumin, ACV, water fresh each time.

---

**Fenugreek Paste Cubes (Hair)**
*Makes 1 tray (~14 cubes) | Shelf life frozen: 4–6 weeks — soak seeds the night before*
1. **Night before:** Soak 4 tbsp fenugreek seeds in 1 cup cold water overnight.
2. **Next day:** Drain completely — discard soaking water.
3. Blend drained seeds with just enough fresh water to form a thick, smooth paste. Start with 2 tbsp water and add more only if needed — it should be the consistency of hummus, not watery.
4. Portion into ice cube tray (~1 tbsp per cube = 1 hair application).
5. Freeze overnight, transfer to labeled container.
6. **Why soak vs. dry powder:** Soaking activates the mucilage and lecithin that coat and condition the hair shaft. Dry fenugreek powder provides significantly less benefit.

## Cadence 3 — Every 2–3 Weeks (Sunday Prep)

Pull ingredients from the 3-month freezer stash. Make all three items in the same Sunday session.

---

**Coconut Chutney Base**
*Makes ~6 servings (3-day supply) | Shelf life frozen: 2–3 weeks*
1. Pull from freezer: 1/3 cup grated coconut, 1 green chili.
2. Thaw coconut and chili at room temp 15 min.
3. Blend: grated coconut + 1 green chili + 1/2 tsp cumin + 1/4 inch fresh ginger + 1 tbsp lemon juice + splash of water or aquafaba + pinch of salt.
4. Blend until smooth. Add water gradually — should be thick, not watery.
5. **No curry leaves** — those go in the tadka cube at serving time, not in the base.
6. Add a small splash of extra water before portioning (base thickens on freeze).
7. Pour into covered ice cube tray. Freeze overnight, transfer to labeled bag.
8. **To use:** Thaw 1–2 cubes at room temp 15 min. Drop 1 curry leaf tadka cube into a warm pan, melt, pour over chutney. Stir well.

---

**Cilantro/Mint TRP Chelation Chutney**
*Makes ~6 servings (3-day supply) | Shelf life frozen: 2–3 weeks signal; 3–4 weeks safety*
1. Pull from freezer: frozen cilantro (4 cups packed), frozen mint (2 cups packed), 4–5 frozen Thai chilies, frozen garlic cloves (4 cloves).
2. Thaw chilies and garlic 10 min. Cilantro and mint can go straight into blender from frozen.
3. Blend: cilantro + mint + Thai chilies + 4 rested garlic cloves + 1 inch ginger + **4 tbsp lemon juice (Acid Lock — critical)** + 1 tsp black salt + 1 tsp roasted cumin.
4. Blend until very smooth.
5. Taste — should be bright, spicy, and tangy. Add more lemon if it tastes flat.
6. Pour into covered ice cube tray. Freeze overnight, transfer to labeled bag.
7. **To use:** Thaw 1–2 cubes at room temp 15 min. Add a fresh squeeze of lemon if flavor seems flat.

The 3-month day needs 11 trays total, but aloe + lemon (8 trays) can go in the freezer overnight together, then imli and aquafaba reuse those trays the next day. **Why silicone:** Flexible so cubes pop out cleanly without cracking the tray, no leaching at freezing temps (leaching is heat-driven), dishwasher safe, lasts indefinitely. Get lidded/covered silicone trays so cubes don't absorb freezer odors.

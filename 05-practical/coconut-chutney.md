# Coconut Chutney (MCT Matrix, Freeze-Stable Base)

The coconut chutney delivers MCT fats (lauric acid, caprylic acid) that bypass standard lipid metabolism, providing immediate ketone energy and elevating thermogenesis [[St-Onge, 2008]](../07-appendices/master-citations.md#10-antioxidants-polyphenols--vascular-signaling). It also acts as the fat matrix that "blooms" fat-soluble spice compounds (capsaicin, curcumin, carbazole alkaloids from curry leaves) for maximum bioavailability.

**Two-tier system:**
- **The Base** — freeze-stable for 3 months, batch once per protocol cycle
- **The Tadka** — fresh curry leaf finish added at serving time, never frozen into the base

---

## 1. Why Separate Base and Tadka

Curry leaves contain volatile carbazole alkaloids [[Balakrishnan, 2020]](../07-appendices/master-citations.md#10-antioxidants-polyphenols--vascular-signaling) that degrade when frozen or blended into a wet base. The aromatic bloom from a fresh tadka is fundamentally different from pre-blended curry leaves — the heat-released volatiles hit the palate immediately rather than being absorbed into the coconut matrix.

**Base:** coconut + ginger + chili + cumin + lemon — all freeze-stable.
**Tadka:** curry leaves + mustard seeds in hot oil — always made fresh at serving.

---

## 2. The Freeze-Stable Base

### Ingredients (makes ~2 cups / ~16 ice-cube portions)

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Fresh grated coconut (or frozen shredded, unsweetened) | 1.5 cups |
| Green chili | 2–3 (adjust to heat tolerance) |
| Fresh ginger | 1 inch knob |
| Roasted cumin powder | 1 tsp |
| Lemon juice (**Acid Lock — mandatory**) | 2 tbsp |
| Water or aquafaba | 1/4 cup (enough to blend smooth) |
| Salt | 1/2 tsp |

### Method

1. **Blend:** Add all ingredients to blender. Blitz until smooth — 60–90 seconds. Add water/aquafaba in small splashes only as needed to get it moving; keep it thick.
2. **Taste check:** Should be rich, coconutty, sharp from ginger and chili, bright from lemon. Adjust salt.
3. **Portion:** Spoon into ice cube trays (~2 tbsp per cube).
4. **Freeze:** 4 hours until solid, then transfer to labeled zip-lock bag.

> **Acid Lock is non-negotiable:** The 2 tbsp lemon juice lowers pH, halts enzymatic browning, and preserves the fresh flavor through the freeze cycle. Without it the base turns grey and loses brightness within days.

## 3. 3-Month Batch Scaling (×8)

| Ingredient | Single batch | 3-month batch (×8) |
|---|---|---|
| Fresh grated coconut | 1.5 cups | **12 cups (~1.2kg)** |
| Green chili | 2–3 | **16–24 chilies** |
| Fresh ginger | 1 inch knob | **8 inch total (~180g)** |
| Roasted cumin powder | 1 tsp | **8 tsp (~2.5 tbsp)** |
| Lemon juice | 2 tbsp | **1 cup (16 tbsp)** |
| Water / aquafaba | 1/4 cup | **2 cups** |
| Salt | 1/2 tsp | **4 tsp** |

**Output:** ~16 cups / ~128 cubes — covers 90 days at 1–2 cubes/day with buffer.

> **Blend in rounds:** Vitamix (64oz) holds ~8 cups comfortably. Split into 2–3 blending rounds, combine in a large bowl before portioning into trays.

---

## 4. The Fresh Tadka (At Serving Time)

Never skip this — it is what makes the chutney taste alive rather than flat.

### Per serving (2 cubes thawed):

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Avocado oil or coconut oil | 1 tsp |
| Fresh curry leaves | 8–10 leaves |
| Mustard seeds | 1/4 tsp |
| Dried red chili (optional) | 1 small |

### Method

1. **Thaw base:** Pull 1–2 cubes, thaw at room temp 15–20 min (or 20 sec microwave).
2. **Heat oil** in a small pan on medium-high until shimmering.
3. **Add mustard seeds** — wait for them to pop (10–15 sec).
4. **Add curry leaves** — they will crackle and spit. Stand back. 15–20 sec until crisp.
5. **Add dried chili** if using — 5 sec.
6. **Pour tadka immediately over thawed base.** Stir to combine.
7. Serve within 5 min — the volatiles dissipate quickly.

> **Frozen curry leaf cubes work here** — if you have frozen curry leaves from batch prep, crumble 4–5 frozen leaves directly into the hot oil. Same effect.

---

## 5. Fresh Herbs at Serving (The Melt Rule)

When you thaw a cube, this is the moment to add any fresh herbs — **never freeze herbs into the base.**

| Herb | Amount per serving | What it adds |
|---|---|---|
| Fresh cilantro (chopped) | 1–2 tbsp | Chelation, brightness, TRP activation |
| Fresh mint (torn) | 4–5 leaves | Menthol cooling, digestion |
| Fresh curry leaves (raw, torn) | 3–4 leaves | Raw carbazole alkaloids (different from tadka — complementary) |

Add after the tadka is poured, stir gently, serve immediately.

---

## 6. Shelf Life

| Format | Stability | Storage |
|---|---|---|
| Frozen base cubes | **3 months** | Zip-lock bag, labeled, freezer |
| Thawed base (no tadka) | 2 days | Covered, fridge |
| Thawed base + tadka | Same day | Serve immediately |
| Fresh 3-day batch (old method) | 3 days | Fridge, airtight glass |

---

## 7. Protocol Usage

*   **12:00 Main Meal:** 1–2 tbsp as a fat replacement alongside patties or samosa. If using 2 tbsp, subtract 1–2 tbsp oil from the 14:30 salad dressing — treat as a fat swap, not an addition.
*   **Pair with:** Vegan patties, tofu, samosa (any variant). The MCT fat matrix blooms the fat-soluble spice compounds in the meal.
*   **Do not pair with:** Iron-absorption window foods — coconut fat slows gastric emptying. Keep lemon juice on the meal to protect iron absorption.

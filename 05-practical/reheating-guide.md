# RS3 Reheating Guide (130°F Ceiling)

The protocol's cook-chill Double-Chill system creates RS3 (Resistant Starch Type 3) crystalline structures in starches and legumes. These structures degrade above **130°F (54°C)**. All reheating must target this ceiling — warm enough to eat, cool enough to preserve the RS3 prebiotic benefit.

> **The rule:** Pull from heat when thermometer reads **125°F**. Carryover heat in a covered dish brings it to 130°F while resting.

---

## Oven Reheating — Black Beans / Chickpeas / Lentils

*Starting temp: fridge (~38°F). Covered oven-safe dish. ~2 cups cooked legumes.*

| Oven Temp | Time to ~125°F (pull point) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **225°F** | **25–35 min** | Ideal — widest safe window, hard to overshoot |
| 250°F | 20–28 min | Still very forgiving |
| 275°F | 18–24 min | Check at 18 min |
| 300°F | 15–20 min | Start checking at 15 min |
| 325°F | 12–16 min | Narrow window — use thermometer |
| 350°F | 10–14 min | Risk of overshooting — not recommended for RS3 preservation |

---

## Oven Reheating — Quinoa / Rice

*Smaller grain mass heats faster than dense legumes.*

| Oven Temp | Time to ~125°F (pull point) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **225°F** | **18–25 min** | Ideal |
| 250°F | 15–20 min | Good |
| 300°F | 10–14 min | Check early |
| 350°F | 7–10 min | Too fast — easy to overshoot |

---

## Oven Reheating — Samosa Filling (Mixed: Beans + Veg)

*Mixed mass — beans are denser, vegetables heat faster.*

| Oven Temp | Time to ~125°F (pull point) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **225°F** | **25–35 min** | Recommended — vegetables won't dry out |
| 250°F | 20–28 min | Good |
| 300°F | 15–20 min | Stir halfway through for even heating |
| 350°F | 12–16 min | Not recommended — veg dries, RS3 risk |

---

## Stovetop Reheating

*Low heat, covered pan, splash of water to prevent sticking.*

| Food | Heat Setting | Time | Method |
|---|---|---|---|
| Black beans / chickpeas | Low | 8–12 min | Stir every 2–3 min, cover between stirs |
| Quinoa | Low | 5–8 min | Add 1–2 tbsp water, cover, stir once |
| Samosa filling | Low–medium low | 8–12 min | Stir frequently, add splash of water |

> **Stovetop tip:** The moment you see steam rising consistently from the pan, you are close to 130°F. Pull immediately.

---

## Microwave Reheating

Microwave creates hot spots — RS3 can be destroyed in patches even if the average temp reads safe. Use short bursts and stir between.

| Food | Method |
|---|---|
| Black beans / chickpeas | 30-sec bursts, stir between, 2–3 rounds max |
| Quinoa | 30-sec burst, stir, 1–2 rounds |
| Samosa filling | 30-sec burst, stir, 2–3 rounds |

> **Not recommended** for RS3-critical meals. Use oven or stovetop when possible. Microwave is a last resort.

---

## The Thermometer Rule

A probe thermometer is the only reliable way to hit the 130°F window consistently. Insert into the **center of the mass** — edges always heat faster.

- **Pull point:** 125°F (carryover brings to 130°F in a covered dish)
- **Hard ceiling:** 140°F — above this, amylopectin RS3 crystallinity begins to reverse
- **Safe eating range:** 110–130°F — warm but not hot

---

## Why This Matters

RS3 forms when cooked starch is chilled and crystallizes. It resists digestion in the small intestine, reaches the colon intact, and feeds butyrate-producing bacteria that repair the gut lining and reduce systemic inflammation. Reheating above 130°F reverses the crystallization — you get regular digestible starch instead of the prebiotic RS3 you spent 24h building.

The Double-Chill batch prep creates RS3. Gentle reheating preserves it. Aggressive reheating destroys it.

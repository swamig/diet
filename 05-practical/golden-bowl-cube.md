# Golden Bowl Cube

A frozen concentrate of **turmeric + black pepper + ginger + cardamom + cinnamon + (optional) ashwagandha**, lipid-locked in avocado oil or ghee, designed to be **stirred into the 17:50 Recovery Bowl** (see [Recovery Bowl](./recovery-bowl.md)) — **NOT** an evening hot-milk drink. The cube is the protocol's second curcumin pulse of the day, layered on top of the ½-tsp turmeric already in the 12:00 salad dressing, and timed to ride the recovery bowl's casein anchor + full-fat yogurt fat carrier for absorption that extends through the overnight fast.

Originally framed as "Golden Milk Cube" (drop into hot milk at 20:30). **Renamed and re-positioned** because a hot-milk drink at 20:30 is incompatible with the protocol's 6-hour TRE window (12:00–18:00) — see the next section.

Follows the design principles in the [Sauce Cubing Master Guide](./sauce-cubing-master-guide.md) — concentrate-and-reduce, acid + lipid lock, dried spices freeze-stable.

---

## The 6-Hour TRE Rule — Why This Goes in the Bowl

The protocol runs a **12:00–18:00 eating window** for the HGH-pulse / autophagy / fasted-IGF-1 stack. Anything past 18:00 is **herbal water only** — see the night-infusion column in the [Daily Timing Cheat Sheet](../07-appendices/daily-timing-cheat-sheet.md). 9 kcal of cumin-coriander-fennel tisane is TRE-tolerant; a cup of hot milk is not.

| Time slot | Caloric load | TRE impact |
|---|---|---|
| 17:50 recovery bowl (yogurt + bowl cube) | ~300 kcal | Inside the window — fine |
| 20:30 hot golden milk | ~150 kcal | **Breaks the fast** — blunts the GH pulse and the early-fasted autophagy window |
| 21:00 night infusion | ~9 kcal herbal | Tolerated · doesn't shift insulin |

The fix: stir the cube into the 17:50 bowl. **Yogurt fat is a better curcumin carrier than warm milk fat anyway** — slower transit, longer mucosal contact, casein anchors the curcumin signal into the overnight fast. The bowl already carries cardamom + cinnamon (phantom sweetness stack), so the cube layers cleanly on top.

## Base Recipe (single batch · ~16 mini cubes · **6-month freeze**)

| Ingredient | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Turmeric (ground)** | 4 tbsp | Curcumin base · the active load |
| **Coarse black pepper** (crack fresh whole peppercorns) | 2 tsp | **1:6 ratio with turmeric — non-negotiable** for 10–20× curcumin bioavailability via piperine |
| Fresh ginger | 2 thumbs (~2 oz) grated | Gingerol · AMPK + Nrf2 secondary pulse |
| Cardamom seeds (from ~15 pods, shells discarded) | 1 tsp | Cineole · parasympathetic + warming |
| Ceylon cinnamon (ground) | ½ tsp | Cinnamaldehyde · stacks with the cinnamon already in the bowl's phantom sweetness layer |
| Ashwagandha root powder (KSM-66) | **1 tsp · OPTIONAL** | Cycle per [supplement-cycling.md](../04-advanced-control/supplement-cycling.md) — 2–3×/wk max. Skip if you want the cube daily-eligible. |
| Sea salt | pinch | Flavor balance + water-activity drop |
| **Avocado oil OR ghee** | 2 tbsp | **Lipid lock — critical** · disperses curcumin uniformly through the cube and carries it through the freeze · see [Sauce Cubing Master Guide §6](./sauce-cubing-master-guide.md#6-acid--lipid-lock-rule) |
| Hot water (~170 °F · **NOT boiling**) | ¼–⅓ cup | Just enough to blend smooth · preserves cardamom volatiles |
| Maple syrup OR date paste | 1 tsp · OPTIONAL | Skip if you don't want sweetness bleeding into the bowl · the phantom sweetness stack already runs cinnamon + cardamom + nutmeg |

**Freeze life: 6 months.** Turmeric + black pepper in a lipid lock is one of the most freeze-stable cube formulations in the system — better than fresh-herb chutneys (3 weeks) or even most reduced glazes (3 months). The dried-spice base has no enzyme-driven signal to lose, and the avocado oil lock prevents the small fresh-ginger fraction from oxidizing.

## Method (~20 min)

1. **Crack whole peppercorns** in a mortar — coarse, not fine. Don't pre-grind earlier in the day; pre-ground pepper loses volatile aromatics ~3× faster than cracked-at-batch.
2. **Crack cardamom pods**, extract seeds, discard shells. The shells are bitter and fibrous; the seeds carry the cineole.
3. **Heat water to ~170 °F** — hot to the touch but not boiling. Boiling destroys cardamom cineole (a volatile terpene that's part of the parasympathetic signal).
4. **Combine dry + wet in a blender** (Vitamix or immersion): turmeric + ginger + cardamom seeds + cinnamon + ashwagandha (if using) + salt.
5. **Pour hot water + oil over** the dry mix.
6. **Blend smooth · 60–90 sec.** The texture should be a thick golden paste — pourable when warm, sets to a soft solid when cool.
7. **Cool slightly.** Pour ~1 tbsp per cell into a mini ice cube tray.
8. **Freeze overnight uncovered.** Transfer to a labeled bag once solid.

**Label:** `Golden Bowl — [date] — use by [date + 6 months]`

## Use Pattern

### Primary: 17:50 Recovery Bowl stir-in

1. **Assemble** the recovery bowl per [Sheet 15](./recovery-bowl.md) — Greek yogurt + berries + casein + black garlic + phantom sweetness pinches.
2. **Drop 1 cube** directly into the bowl. Let it sit ~30 sec to thaw and warm in the yogurt.
3. **Stir thoroughly** until the cube fully dissolves (~60 sec). Under-stirred = curcumin pockets that don't get the yogurt fat carrier.
4. **Eat immediately** — curcumin + casein + yogurt fat combo absorbs over the next 6–8 hours, peak Nrf2 / NF-κB activity right through the overnight fast.

**Color note:** turmeric yellow + blueberry purple = **muddy brown**. Either accept this (some users find the brown-orange tone telegraphs "anti-inflammatory" and lean into it) or sub blackberries (purple-black blends with turmeric-yellow into a deep mahogany that looks intentional). See the color-compatibility table below.

### Secondary (optional): 09:15 matcha-day morning drink

- Drop 1 cube into hot matcha + water at 09:15 — tea-style, whisk to dissolve.
- ~30 kcal — **borderline TRE-tolerant** ("dirty fasting" interpretation).
- **Strict TRE:** skip; use bowl only.
- **Liberal TRE:** fine, especially on rest days when the GH pulse matters less.

### Never use at 21:00 night infusion

The night infusion is **herbal water only** — see [Hydration](../01-foundations/04-hydration.md) and the night-infusion column in the [Daily Timing Cheat Sheet](../07-appendices/daily-timing-cheat-sheet.md). The cube has 30+ kcal of lipid and would break the fast.

## Bio-Signal Pathways

| Compound | Pathway | Window of effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Curcumin × piperine** | **Nrf2 activation + NF-κB suppression** | Plasma curcumin peaks ~6–8 hr after ingestion — right inside the overnight fast for a 17:50 dose |
| Gingerol | AMPK + Nrf2 secondary pulse | Compounds the curcumin signal; faster onset (~2 hr peak) |
| Cardamom cineole | Anti-inflammatory + mild parasympathetic | Aids sleep onset · stacks with the cardamom already in the bowl |
| Cinnamaldehyde | Glycemic anchor + olfactory sweetness | Stacks with the Ceylon cinnamon already in the bowl's phantom sweetness layer |
| Ashwagandha withanolides (optional) | HPA-axis modulation · cortisol AUC drop | Cycles per [supplement-cycling.md](../04-advanced-control/supplement-cycling.md) — 2–3×/wk max |

## Frequency

- **Daily eligible** without ashwagandha. Curcumin doesn't down-regulate at 1 cube/day; the piperine multiplier holds steady week over week.
- **2–3×/wk if WITH ashwagandha** — chronic daily ashwagandha → HPA-axis receptor down-regulation. Cycle per the [supplement-cycling](../04-advanced-control/supplement-cycling.md) ashwagandha rule.
- **5-on / 1-off** is a sensible default rhythm if you want a built-in reset cadence; not required.

**Practical compromise:** make **two parallel batches** — one without ashwagandha (daily bag, ~16 cubes) and one with (M/W/F bag, ~8 cubes). Label distinctly so you don't accidentally double-pulse ashwagandha across days.

## 3-Month Vitamix Batch (8× → ~128 cubes)

One Vitamix run handles the full 8× volume (~8 cups working capacity).

| Item | Total |
|---|---|
| Turmeric (ground) | ~1 cup (~120 g) |
| Black pepper, coarse-cracked | ¼ cup ground worth |
| Fresh ginger | ~16 thumbs (~1 lb) |
| Cardamom seeds (from ~120 pods) | 8 tsp |
| Ceylon cinnamon | 4 tsp |
| Ashwagandha (optional · parallel bag) | 2–3 tbsp |

| Hot water (~170 °F) | 2–3 cups |
| Sea salt | 1 tsp |

**Method:** same as single batch, but the Vitamix holds the full 8× volume in one go. Blend smooth · pour into mini trays · freeze overnight · bag and label. ~128 mini cubes ≈ ~4 months at one cube per recovery bowl (~6 bowls/week).

## Critical Rules

- **Turmeric stains everything.** Use a silicone tray you don't mind yellowing, a dedicated cutting board if you're grating fresh turmeric instead of using ground, and an apron. The stain on plastic trays is permanent.
- **1:6 pepper:turmeric ratio is non-negotiable.** That's the piperine boost for curcumin bioavailability (~10–20×). Don't reduce pepper for taste — if it's too sharp, dilute with more cube volume, don't cut the pepper.
- **Crack whole peppercorns at batch time.** Pre-ground pepper loses volatile aromatics ~3× faster than cracked-at-batch.
- **Avocado oil OR ghee, not coconut oil.** Coconut oil solidifies at fridge temp and the cube becomes hard to pour cleanly; the mouthfeel goes waxy when stirred into cold yogurt. Avocado oil and ghee stay supple at freezer temp and emulsify cleanly with the yogurt fat.
- **170 °F water max.** Boiling destroys cardamom cineole — the volatile terpene that carries the parasympathetic signal. Hot enough to bloom the spices, not hot enough to drive off aromatics.
- **Stir the cube thoroughly into the bowl.** Under-stirred = curcumin pockets that don't get the yogurt fat carrier. Aim for uniform color across the bowl, no streaks.

## Recovery Bowl Color Compatibility

The bowl already runs blueberries by default ([Sheet 15](./recovery-bowl.md)). Adding turmeric-yellow on top creates predictable color outcomes:

| Bowl base | Golden Bowl Cube fit | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Yogurt + blueberries | Acceptable | Muddy purple-brown · taste fine, looks off-putting |
| Yogurt + **blackberries** | **Best** | Blackberry-black + turmeric-yellow → deep mahogany · looks intentional |
| Yogurt + cherries | Acceptable | Red + yellow → orange · taste compatible |
| Yogurt + strawberries | **Avoid** | Red + yellow + white → baby-food pink · highest ugliness factor |
| Plain yogurt | **Best** | Pure golden bowl · phantom sweetness stack only · no berry color clash |

**Practical:** on Golden Bowl days, swap blueberries for blackberries. The anthocyanin profile is comparable (cyanidin-3-glucoside is in both), and the visual is dramatically better.

## Compared to Other Curcumin Pulses

The protocol now runs **up to three curcumin pulses per day** at full stack:

| Slot | Source | Approx curcumin dose | Pathway / carrier |
|---|---|---|---|
| 12:00 salad dressing ([daily-salad-layer.md](./daily-salad-layer.md)) | ½ tsp turmeric sprinkled on wet dressing + cracked pepper | ~50 mg | Olive oil carrier + piperine — fast pulse |
| 17:50 recovery bowl (**this cube**) | ¼ tsp turmeric per cube + 1:6 piperine | **~100 mg** | Yogurt fat + casein anchor — slow pulse through overnight fast |
| 09:15 matcha (optional) | Same cube in hot matcha + water | ~100 mg | Borderline TRE-tolerant — strict TRE skips this |

**Daily total at full stack:** ~150–250 mg curcumin. Well below the 4–8 g/day upper safety limit. The compounded Nrf2 + NF-κB effect from multi-pulse dosing exceeds a single-pulse dose of the same total amount — the receptor activation has a refractory window of ~6 hr, so spaced pulses keep the signaling envelope wider through the day.

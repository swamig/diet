# Tamarind Chutney (Imli-Khajur, Freeze-Stable)

A sweet-sour-spiced dipping chutney using Tropica concentrated tamarind and Medjool dates as the sweetener base. Designed for batch production and 6-month freeze stability.

**Default:** date-sweetened (lower GI, adds iron + fiber, better body).
**Variant:** jaggery-sweetened (traditional, thinner consistency — see Section 5).

---

## 1. Ingredients (makes ~2 cups / ~16 ice-cube portions)

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Medjool dates (pitted) | 10–12 |
| Tropica concentrated tamarind | 3 tbsp |
| Water | 1.5 cups |
| Salt | 1 tsp |
| Black salt (kala namak) | 1/2 tsp |

> **Tropica concentrate note:** Tropica is ~3–4× more concentrated than soaked raw block tamarind. Do not substitute 1:1 with regular tamarind pulp recipes.

---

## 2. Spice Stack

| Spice | Amount | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Roasted cumin powder | 2 tsp | Core flavor, digestive |
| Ginger (see Section 4 for fresh vs. dry) | 1 tsp powder / 1.5–2 inch knob | Warmth, anti-nausea |
| Fennel powder (saunf) | 1/2 tsp | Sweetness, rounds acid — *see substitution note below* |
| Red chili powder | 1/2 tsp | Heat |
| Cinnamon | 1/4 tsp | Bridges dates + tamarind |
| Black pepper | 1/4 tsp | Depth, piperine |
| Hing (asafoetida) | 1/8 tsp | Anti-flatulent, complexity |

---

## 3. Method

1. **Blend the base:** Add pitted dates + water to a blender. Blitz until completely smooth — no chunks.
2. **Combine:** Pour date paste into a saucepan. Add Tropica tamarind and stir to dissolve.
3. **Spice in:** Add all spices + salts.
4. **Cook:** Medium heat, stirring frequently, 15–20 min until it thickens to a slow-dripping ribbon consistency (coats the back of a spoon).
5. **Taste check:** Should hit sweet-sour-spiced in equal measure. Add more tamarind if too sweet, a pinch of salt if flat.
6. **Optional strain:** Push through a mesh strainer for a silky smooth texture — removes date fiber pulp. Skip if you don't mind a slightly textured chutney.
7. Cool completely before portioning.

> **Consistency note:** The chutney looks slightly thinner while hot — it thickens as it cools. If it sets too thick from the fridge, thin with a few drops of water when serving.

## 4. Ginger Variant — Fresh vs. Dry

| | Dry ginger powder (sonth) | Fresh ginger (wet) |
|---|---|---|
| Amount | 1 tsp | 1.5–2 inch knob, grated or microplaned |
| Character | Warm, concentrated, pungent | Bright, citrusy, sharper |
| When to add | With the spices (step 3) | Blend directly into dates (step 1) |
| Freeze stability | Excellent | Good — loses some brightness but holds well |
| Result | Classic chutney warmth | Slightly zingier, still excellent |

**Fennel substitution options** (in order of preference):

| Option | Amount (single batch) | Amount (3-month ×6) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Star anise (ground)** *(preferred)* | **1/16 tsp** | **3/8 tsp** | Same anise family, more depth — bridges the cinnamon note. Grind 1–2 whole points. ~4–5× more potent than fennel, do not exceed. |
| Whole fennel seeds (ground) | 1/2 tsp | 3 tsp | Dry roast 2–3 min, then grind — identical to fennel powder |
| Cardamom | 1/4 tsp | 1.5 tsp | Different flavor, adds sweetness bridge |
| Skip entirely | — | — | Chutney still works — fennel is supporting, not load-bearing |

**Best practice with fresh ginger:** Blend it into the dates in step 1. The fiber integrates into the base and, if you strain in step 6, the pulp comes out cleanly — leaving only the flavor behind.

Both work. Dry ginger is the traditional choice; fresh ginger is a solid substitute with a slightly different personality.

## 5. Jaggery Variant (Traditional)

Replace dates with jaggery for a thinner, more traditional consistency.

| Ingredient change | Amount |
|---|---|
| Remove: Medjool dates | — |
| Add: Jaggery (grated) | 80–100g |
| Water | 2 cups (more water needed — no date body) |

**Method change:** Dissolve jaggery directly in water in the saucepan (no blending step). Add tamarind + spices and cook down 15–20 min.

**Differences from the date version:**
- Thinner, more pourable consistency
- Less body — won't thicken as much
- Slightly higher glycemic (no fiber buffer)
- Trace minerals (iron, potassium) from the molasses in jaggery
- Flavor: slightly more caramel/molasses vs. the deeper dried-fruit note from dates

## 6. Vitamix Sizing — 3-Month Batch (Do Twice)

The ×3 single batch is the ideal Vitamix load — one run fills the container correctly and produces ~48 cubes. **Do it twice** for the full 3-month supply (~96 cubes).

**Per Vitamix run (×3 single batch):**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Medjool dates (pitted) | **30–36 dates (~600g)** |
| Water | **4.5 cups** |
| Fresh ginger (blend in with dates) | **5 tsp grated (~5–6 inch knob)** |

Blend dates + water + ginger until completely smooth. Pour into saucepan, add tamarind + spices, cook down 15–20 min.

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Tropica concentrated tamarind | **9 tbsp (~1/3 jar)** |
| Salt | **1 tbsp** |
| Black salt | **1.5 tsp** |
| Roasted cumin powder | **2 tbsp** |
| Star anise ground *(or fennel)* | **~1/4 tsp *(or 1.5 tsp)*** |
| Red chili powder | **1.5 tsp** |
| Cinnamon | **3/4 tsp** |
| Black pepper | **3/4 tsp** |
| Hing | **3/8 tsp** |
| Turmeric | **1/4 tsp** |

**Output per run:** ~48 cubes
**× 2 runs:** ~96 cubes = 3-month supply (1/3 jar tamarind per run, 2/3 jar total) ✓

## 7. 3-Month Batch Scaling (Reference Table)

Scale ×6 for a full 90-day supply (~96 cubes, 1/day with slight buffer).

| Ingredient | Single batch | 3-month batch (×6) |
|---|---|---|
| Medjool dates (pitted) | 10–12 | **60–72 dates (~1.2kg)** |
| Tropica concentrated tamarind | 3 tbsp | **18 tbsp (~270ml, ~1 jar)** |
| Water | 1.5 cups | **9 cups** |
| Salt | 1 tsp | **2 tbsp** |
| Black salt (kala namak) | 1/2 tsp | **1 tbsp** |
| Roasted cumin powder | 2 tsp | **4 tbsp** |
| Fresh ginger | 1.5–2 inch knob | **~200g total** |
| Star anise (ground) *(or fennel)* | 1/16 tsp *(or 1/2 tsp)* | **3/8 tsp *(or 3 tsp)*** |
| Red chili powder | 1/2 tsp | **1 tbsp** |
| Cinnamon | 1/4 tsp | **1.5 tsp** |
| Black pepper | 1/4 tsp | **1.5 tsp** |
| Hing | 1/8 tsp | **3/4 tsp** |

> **Do not cook in one pot.** Split into 2–3 batches on the stove — a single large volume won't reduce or thicken evenly. Combine after cooking, then freeze.

---

## 7. Freeze Protocol (3-Month Stability)

The high acid (tamarind pH ~2.5) + high natural sugar (dates) creates a hostile environment for microbial growth — the same reason traditional tamarind concentrate keeps for months at room temperature. Frozen, shelf life is effectively 6 months.

1. Pour cooled chutney into **ice cube trays** (~2 tbsp per cube)
2. Freeze solid (~4 hours)
3. Pop cubes into a zip-lock bag, label with date
4. **To thaw:** One cube at room temp for 20 min, or 30 seconds in the microwave

Each cube = one serving.
